# Automatic Creation of AI Cases and Inquiries from Inbound Email

## Overview

The system automatically creates **AI cases** and **inquiries** from inbound emails by using keywords in the subject line and body, based on configured email intake settings.

This feature is part of the **Exploring AI Risk and Compliance** chapter of the ServiceNow documentation. It enables email-based intake as an alternative submission channel for AI-related cases and inquiries.

## What Is Email-Based Intake?

Email-based intake provides a simple and familiar way for users to submit AI-related cases or inquiries. Instead of navigating a portal or completing forms, users simply send an email to configured addresses.

### How It Works

When an email is received, the system:

1. Processes the message.
2. Extracts key information such as the **subject**, **body**, and **sender**.
3. Creates an **AI Case** or **AI Inquiry** record.

This automation helps capture submissions consistently and immediately, making them ready for review and action.

> For step-by-step configuration instructions, see **Configure email-based intake for AI Risk and Compliance**.

## Benefits of Email-Based Intake

This capability offers the following benefits:

- Users can submit AI-related concerns or questions using a familiar communication method.
- Reduces manual data entry and helps minimize reporting errors.
- Helps confirm that each submission is captured as a trackable record.
- Improves visibility by making new cases and inquiries immediately available for review.

## Email-to-ServiceNow Field Mapping

When an inbound email is processed, information from the email is used to populate fields on the resulting AI Case or AI Inquiry record.

| Email Element | Mapped ServiceNow Field | Notes |
|---------------|-------------------------|-------|
| Email sender | **Requester** (both record types) | Depends on inbound email configuration and trusted domain settings. When automatic user creation is disabled, the Requester field displays **Guest**. |
| Email sent date | **Date of Discovery** (AI cases) / **Created on** (AI inquiries) | Different target field depending on record type. |
| TO recipient | **Assignment group** (both record types) | The recipient address determines the assignment group. |
| CC recipients | **Watch list** | Any CC recipients are added to the watch list. |
| Email importance | **Priority** (case or inquiry) | See priority mapping below. |
| Email subject | **Name** | The subject is set as the record name. |
| Email body | **Description** | |
| Email attachments | Linked to the record | Attachments are included and linked. |
| (AI cases only) | **Source** | Set to **Email**. |

### Requester Field Behavior

- The email sender is mapped to the **Requester** field for both record types.
- This behavior depends on **inbound email configuration** and **trusted domain settings**.
- When **automatic user creation is disabled**, the Requester field displays **Guest**.

### Priority Mapping by Email Importance

Email importance determines the case or inquiry priority:

| Email Importance | Resulting Priority |
|------------------|--------------------|
| High importance | Priority 2 (High) |
| Normal importance | Priority 3 (Moderate) |
| Low importance | Priority 4 (Low) |

## Key Points

- Both **AI Cases** and **AI Inquiries** can be created automatically from inbound email.
- Record creation is driven by **keywords in the subject line and body** and by **configured email intake settings**.
- The behavior of certain mappings (notably the Requester field) is dependent on inbound email and trusted domain configuration.
- For AI cases, the **Source** field is explicitly set to **Email**, distinguishing email-originated cases from other intake channels.
