# AI Risk and Compliance Workspace

The **AI Risk and Compliance workspace** enables the **AI risk and compliance manager** to view the complete risk posture of the AI assets for an enterprise.

## Why a Full View of AI Risk Matters

An AI risk and compliance manager must have a full view of the risks associated with AI assets like AI systems, models, and datasets for several important reasons:

- **Comprehensive risk assessment:** Having a complete understanding of all potential risks enables the manager to conduct a thorough risk assessment. This includes identifying vulnerabilities, potential failures, and areas where the AI could cause harm or non-compliance.
- **Regulatory compliance:** Many industries have specific regulations and standards that AI systems must adhere to. A full view of the risks helps ensure that the AI assets comply with these regulations, avoiding legal penalties and maintaining the organization's reputation.
- **Data privacy and security:** AI systems often handle sensitive data. Understanding the risks helps in implementing robust security measures to protect this data from breaches, unauthorized access, and misuse.
- **Operational efficiency:** By identifying and mitigating risks, the manager can improve the overall efficiency and reliability of AI systems. This reduces downtime, errors, and the need for costly corrections.
- **Issue management:** In the event of an issue, having a full view of the risks allows for a more effective and rapid response. The manager can quickly identify the root cause and take appropriate actions to mitigate the impact.

## Accessing the Workspace

Navigate to **All > AI Risk and Compliance Workspace**.

The home page meets the requirements above and displays relevant data across the following tabs:

- Risk and compliance dashboard
- Operations dashboard
- AI cases dashboard

## Managed vs. Unmanaged Assets (Applies to All Dashboards)

> **Note:** Dashboards in the AI Risk and Compliance Workspace (Risk and Compliance, Operations, and AI Cases) display data for **Managed AI assets only**. AI assets marked as **Unmanaged** are excluded from dashboard widgets, metrics, and summary counts, but remain visible in inventory and list views. If you have the **AI steward [sn_ai_governance_ai_steward]** role, you can mark assets as Managed or Unmanaged. For more information, see *Assets list managed and unmanaged assets*.

## What Are AI Assets?

AI assets refer to the various components and resources essential for the development, deployment, and operation of artificial intelligence systems:

1. **AI systems:** The complete software or hardware infrastructure that runs AI algorithms and processes. This can include machine learning platforms, natural language processing systems, and other AI-driven applications.
2. **AI models:** The mathematical and computational models trained on data to perform specific tasks. These range from simple linear regression models to complex deep learning neural networks.
3. **Datasets:** The collections of data used to train, validate, and test AI models.

Understanding and managing these AI assets is crucial for ensuring AI systems are effective, reliable, and compliant with regulatory and ethical standards as defined by your organization.

## Risk & Compliance Tab

The **Risk & compliance** tab displays the risk classification of an AI asset inventory and the compliance posture for the selected authority documents and policies. You can drill down into the data on each widget in any section.

### Compliance Overview

#### Regulatory risk classification
Displays the risk classifications of **AI systems, AI models, and Datasets** using **donut charts**. Risks are qualitatively classified as **High, Low, Medium, and Unacceptable**, based on the risk assessments of the AI assets.

#### Compliance by authority documents and policies
Shows compliance based on controls implemented. By default, compliance scores are displayed for the following frameworks provided in the library:

| Framework | Details |
|---|---|
| **NIST AI Risk Management Framework** | Displays four key associated citations: **map, measure, manage, and govern**. Each citation's compliance score is displayed based on its control attestations. |
| **EU Artificial Intelligence Act** | Has multiple chapters displayed as **citations and child citations**. Each citation is mapped to a control objective to provide a compliance percentage score. |

You can:
- View compliance data by selecting one of two options: **Authority Documents** or **Policies**.
- View the overall compliance score percentage, along with the number of compliant and non-compliant authority documents and policies, using the **drop-down filter** to select specific authority documents or policies.
- See all the issues that require immediate attention and AI cases related to each authority document or policy.

> **Disclaimer:** The authority documents are provided solely for informational and guidance purposes to assist with the initial setup of AI Risk and Compliance frameworks. They do not constitute legal advice or assurance of regulatory compliance. You are solely responsible for ensuring that all use of the content complies with applicable laws, regulations, directives, and industry standards in your jurisdiction.

> **Note:** You can configure which authority documents and policies you want to display on the home page. For more information, see *Set up properties for compliance posture*.

### Risk Overview
Monitors and tracks the risk posture of the AI assets in your organization. Using the **AI asset filter**, you can filter risk posture insights by the type of AI asset inventory.

#### AI systems by aggregated risk score
Displays the classifications of AI systems by aggregated risk score using a **donut chart**. Risk scores are qualitatively classified as **High and Low**.

#### Risk heatmap
Displays the visualization of all identified risks within the AI assets.
- By default, a **residual risk** filter is applied, but you can filter based on **inherent risk** level.
- The heatmap is segmented, and the segmentation changes based on the filter.
- Activities fall under the respective combination of **risk and control effectiveness**, or **impact and likelihood** — the combination is based on the selected risk classification filter.
- You can filter the risk heatmap by **Risk Assessment Methodology (RAM)** if you have more than one risk RAM published.

### Regulatory Landscape Overview
> Requires the **GRC: Regulatory Change Management** application to be installed. For more information, see *Installing Regulatory Change Management*.

#### Overview
- **Alerts:** Displays the distribution of regulatory alerts by workflow state or life cycle phase using a **donut chart**.
- **Change tasks:** Displays the distribution of regulatory change tasks by workflow state or life cycle phase using a **donut chart**.

#### Assessments
Displays the regulatory impact assessments and risk assessments linked to regulatory changes using a **donut chart**. By default, the **regulatory assessments** filter is applied, but you can change it to **risk assessments**.

## Operations Tab

The **Operations** tab provides an overview of AI systems by state and department. It shows risk assessments, AI assessments, control assurance, and a breakdown of issues by priority and policy exceptions by risk rating. It also shows the number of AI cases by priority. You can drill down into the data on each widget.

### AI systems by state
Displays the number of AI systems by state:

| State | Description |
|---|---|
| **New** | Submitted/registered for governance and in early intake (pre-build) stages. |
| **Assess** | Governance assessments are in progress or under review (for example, impact or high-risk assessments). |
| **Build** | In development/build-and-test, including control implementation and related governance tasks. |
| **Review for deployment** | Pre-deployment review to confirm assessments are complete and key issues/exceptions are addressed. |
| **Live and Monitor** | Deployed and under ongoing governance monitoring for risks, issues, and compliance signals. |
| **Offboard** | In the retired/offboarding state. Governance reviews may be completed during this state before the asset life cycle is finalized as retired. Available substates depend on configuration and appear in the UI. |

### Assessments

| Widget | Description |
|---|---|
| **Risk assessments** | Displays the number of assessments that are open, in progress, overdue, and due in 7 days. |
| **AI assessments** | Displays the number of assessments in draft, assigned, and work in progress states. Also shows the number of assessments that are open, overdue, and due in 7 days. |

### Control assurance

| Widget | Description |
|---|---|
| **Attestations** | Displays the number of attestations that are open and overdue. Drill down to view the list of control attestations awaiting response collection. Apply the search filter to view **Control Attestations powered by Smart Assessment** (select **New**) or **Control Attestations powered by Classic Survey** (select **Classic**) from the drop-down menu. |
| **Indicators** | Displays the number of indicators that are open and those that failed in the last 6 months. |

### Tracking

| Widget | Description |
|---|---|
| **Issues** | View the number of issues that are open, overdue, and due in 7 days. Also view the pie chart of issues by priority. |
| **Policy exceptions** | View the number of policy exceptions that are open, overdue, and due in 7 days. Also view the pie chart of policy exceptions by risk rating. |
| **AI cases** | View the number of AI cases that are active, overdue, and due in 7 days. Also view the pie chart of AI cases by priority. |

## AI Cases Tab

Track, monitor, and analyze your AI case workflows, identify workflow bottlenecks, and monitor accountability of AI-related risks using the AI risk and compliance dashboard. As an **AI steward**, you can also track the status and trends of AI-related inquiries.

The AI cases tab provides key metrics, trends, and actionable tasks to track AI risks, case activities, compliance tracking, and governance enforcement. You get case-level insights, task-level tracking, and accountability for responsibilities and progress.

- As an **AI steward**, you can report a case when a violation is found.
- An **AI analyst** follows the case and comes up with a resolution.

You can drill down into the data on each of the following widgets:

### AI cases status by state
Shows distribution of reported AI cases by life cycle state or workflow state.

### AI cases status by priority
AI cases based on priority: **Critical, High, Moderate, Low, and Planning**.

### AI cases
Summarized list of AI cases reported:

- **All:** All the AI cases
- **Overdue:** AI cases past the due date
- **Due in 7 days:** AI cases due in the next 7 days
- **Unassigned:** AI cases not assigned to anyone

Attributes of each case — **Name, Sub-type, Case analyst, Priority, and State** — are available in a list view. Select each card to view that category of AI cases in a list view.

### Trends
Visual patterns and historical data associated with AI cases:

- **AI cases by subtype widget:** Trends by different categories of AI case subtypes. Group data by **business unit, location, or primary cause** for contextual insight into issue/AI case patterns across departments or systems. Filterable by AI assets.
- **Open and closed cases widget:** Historical trends of case resolutions over a specified time frame (last 12 months). Helps stakeholders analyze volume fluctuations and case-closure efficiency.
  - *Example:* In the last 12 months, 4,200 cases were created and 3,960 were closed. Average closure time was 2.8 days; peak case volume was January 2025 (510 cases created); fastest resolution month was March 2025. Over the past 12 months, case closure efficiency steadily improved, with a 12% decrease in average resolution time compared to the previous year.

### Tracking
AI case progress at the task and issue level:

- **Action tasks**
  - Number of tasks in the Overdue, Due in 7 days, or Unassigned states.
  - Task distribution by status, such as Draft or Work in Progress.
- **Issues**
  - Counts of issues linked to AI cases by their states like New, Review, or Responds.
  - Counts of issues linked to AI cases by additional details like Overdue, Due in 7 days, or Unassigned.

### Actions
- **Create AI case button:** Initiates a new AI case record within the monitoring dashboard. Typically used to document, investigate, or track issues, risks, or compliance concerns related to AI models or datasets.
- **Create issue button:** Creates an issue related to AI cases.

## Inquiries Tab

The **Inquiries** tab provides a structured view of the AI inquiry workflow. Use the widgets to track compliance and to monitor the status and progress of an inquiry. You can drill down into the data on each widget.

### Inquiries status by state
Counts of inquiries by state — **New, Triage, In progress, and Approved** — represented in a **donut chart**.

### Inquiries status by priority
Counts of inquiries by priority. An inquiry can have **Critical, High, Moderate, Low, or Planning** priorities.

### Inquiries
Summarized list of all AI inquiries, filterable by parameters such as status, priority, and assignment. Each inquiry entry includes metadata such as:

- Name
- Requested by
- Assigned to
- State
- Priority

### Trends
Trend-based insights into inquiry activity across the organization, supporting longitudinal analysis to improve planning and compliance responsiveness.

> **Note:** Longitudinal analysis refers to analyzing data points across departments, teams, or systems over a period of time.

Filters such as **AI assets** and **time range** help refine the analytics.

- **Inquiries by business unit:** Shows distribution of inquiries across departments or functional areas, helping identify where most AI-related concerns originate so you can plan targeted risk mitigation or awareness training.
- **Open and closed inquiries:** Tracks the volume of inquiries initiated and resolved within a selected time range, enabling you to monitor workflow performance and plan for future capacity.

### Actions
- **Create inquiry button:** Initiates a new inquiry, enabling timely reports of AI-related concerns or clarification requests.
