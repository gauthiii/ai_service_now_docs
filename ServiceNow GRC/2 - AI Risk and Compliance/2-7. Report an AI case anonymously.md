# Report an AI Case Anonymously

Report an AI case by describing the issue, affected users, and any relevant data. You can report anytime, without sharing your name or contact details. Investigators only see the report details, not who submitted it.

## Before You Begin

- **Application requirement:** Anonymous AI case reporting is available when the **AI Case Management (`sn_ai_case_mgmt`)** application is installed. Installing AI Case Management automatically installs and configures the **Anonymous Report Center** plugin and the required record producers.
- **Role required:** `sn_ai_case_mgmt.ai_case_business_user` — this role is required to access the **Employee Center**.
  - **No role is required** to submit a report directly through the Anonymous Report Center.

## About This Task

The AI case is managed as part of the **AI Risk and Compliance** application and can be viewed in **AI Control Tower** to support risk and compliance oversight.

The **Report an AI case** record producer within the AI Case Management application (`sn_ai_case_mgmt`) supports secure handling of anonymous AI case reports. Before an anonymous report is saved, all free-text fields (such as case type, summary, business unit, and jurisdiction) are automatically **sanitized**. This process:

- Removes HTML, scripts, styles, symbols, and unsupported characters.
- Restricts input to standard alphanumeric characters and basic punctuation.
- Trims extra whitespace.
- Retains **only** alphanumeric characters and `@ . _ -`.

These automated checks help reduce the risk of unsafe or malformed input in anonymously submitted AI cases.

> **Note:** When submitting an anonymous report, don't include personal data about yourself or other individuals that could directly or indirectly reveal your identity (for example, names, email addresses, or phone numbers).

## Procedure

### 1. Access the Anonymous Report Center

Use one of the following options.

| Option | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Direct portal access** | a. Launch the portal by using `[your instance URL]/arc`.<br>b. Select **Submit a report anonymously**. |
| **Employee center access** | a. Navigate to **All > Self-Service > Employee Center**.<br>b. On the Employee Center landing page, select **Help center > Technology services**, then select **AI assets** from the Technology services topics section.<br>c. Select the **Visit Anonymous Report Center** card.<br>d. Select **Submit a report anonymously**. |

If you're unable to access the Anonymous Report Center, confirm the following:

- Confirm with a team member that has the **admin** role that AI Case Management is installed and active.
- Confirm that pop-up blockers or browser extensions aren't preventing the portal from loading.

> After accessing the Anonymous Report Center, you're **signed out automatically** to keep your identity anonymous.

### 2. Select Report an AI case and fill in the form

Select **Report an AI case** and, on the form, fill in the fields. For full descriptions of the fields, see the **Report an AI case form** table below.

### 3. Enter the CAPTCHA

Enter the CAPTCHA verification code shown on the screen.

### 4. Submit

Select **Submit**.

After you submit an anonymous AI case, the system generates a **report number** and a **private report key**. Save this information securely.

- You need the **report key** to check status updates or provide additional information later.
- If you lose the report key, **it can't be recovered** and the case can't be identified or retrieved on your behalf.
- You need this information to follow up on a case (see *Follow Up on a Report* below).

### 5. Download a copy (optional)

To save a PDF copy of your report, select **Download report copy**.

## Result

The AI case record is created and assigned to the **AI case team** for review. A report extract is generated so you can follow up on the progress of the submitted AI case.

> **Note:** Maintain this report extract securely for future reference to preserve anonymity.

## What to Do Next

- A user with the **AI Risk and Compliance Analyst** role (`sn_grc_ai_gov.ai_risk_and_compliance_analyst`) who is part of the **Assigned group** (according to the Assignment rules) handles the case.
- AI cases are managed through **AI Case Management** within **AI Risk and Compliance** to help ensure consistent tracking, investigation, and resolution of AI-related issues.
- After an AI case is created and enters the **New** state, the next step is **Triage**, where the AI Risk and Compliance team reviews the case to validate scope, assess severity, and determine the appropriate course of action. Depending on the outcome of triage, the team may initiate an assessment. For more information, see *Initiate AI assessment on an AI asset*.

## Report an AI Case Form

Use the **Report an AI case** form in the Anonymous Report Center to report an AI case with the necessary details. After you submit the AI case report, **mandatory fields and input format are validated** before submission.

> **Note:** When submitting an anonymous report, don't include personal data about yourself or other individuals that could directly or indirectly reveal identity (for example, names, email addresses, or phone numbers).

### Basic information

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Type of AI issue** | Category of the AI issue being reported. **Mandatory.** Options are listed in the table below. |
| **Brief summary of the AI case** | Short, descriptive summary of the concern. For example, "Incorrect AI recommendation shown to customers." **Mandatory.** |
| **Date you became aware of this event** | Date when you first became aware of the AI-related issue. By default, the current system date populates this field. Input the date in the format `YYYY-MM-DD`. The date provided cannot be a future date. |
| **Describe what happened in a few words** | Provide a detailed description of the AI case. Explain what happened, why it may involve improper or risky use of AI, and any potential impact on the organization, customers, or operations. Anonymous reports accept standard text characters only — unsupported characters (including spaces, symbols, scripts, and code) are automatically removed during submission. Only alphanumeric characters and `@ . _ -` are retained. **Mandatory.** |
| **Rate this case as criticality** | Indicate the severity or criticality level of the AI case. Options: Critical, High, Moderate, Low, Planning. |

#### "Type of AI issue" options

| Option | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Fairness & Transparency | Lack of explainability in AI decision-making, leading to unfair outcomes. |
| Misinformation & Deepfake | AI-generated false content violating copyrights, patents, or trademarks. |
| IP Theft & Copyright Infringement | AI-generated content violating copyrights, patents, or trademarks. |
| Unauthorised AI Model Usage | Use of AI models outside agreed licensing terms. |
| AI System Malfunction | AI failures leading to unintended consequences in critical applications. |
| Data Poisoning | Manipulation of training data to mislead AI model behavior. |
| Bias & Discrimination | e.g., racial, gender, socioeconomic biases. |
| Model Inversion & Extraction | Unauthorised access or reverse engineering of AI models. |
| AI-induced Physical or Psychological Harm | Physical harm example – self-driving car accidents. Psychological impact example – misinformation. |
| Adversarial Attacks | Deliberate manipulation of AI models to produce incorrect results. |
| Privacy & Data Protection Cases | Unauthorised collection, processing, or misuse of personal data. |
| Autonomy & Human Oversight | AI acting independently without appropriate human intervention, causing ethical concerns. |

### Nature of impact

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Business units involved or impacted** | Business units affected by the AI case. For example, HR, Operations, Customer Service, or Risk & Compliance. |
| **Departments involved or impacted** | Departments affected by the AI case, such as Marketing, Legal, or Operations. Enter "Skip if unknown" if applicable. |
| **Location/jurisdiction** | Location where the event occurred, such as an office site, department area, internal system, application, or customer-facing environment. |
| **Text verification** | Enter the CAPTCHA verification code on the screen. |

## Follow Up on a Report from the Anonymous Reporting Center

After you submit an anonymous report, save the **Report key** and **Report number**. Use these reference numbers to follow up or address comments on your case anonymously.

### Before you begin

- **Role required:** `sn_ai_case_mgmt.ai_case_business_user` (required to access the Employee Center). No role is required to submit a report directly through the Anonymous Report Center.

### About this task

You can report anytime, without sharing your name or contact details. Investigators only see the report details, not who submitted it. The AI case is managed as part of AI Risk and Compliance and can be viewed in AI Control Tower.

When you submitted your anonymous AI case, the system generated a **report number** and a **private report key**. You need the report key to check status updates or provide additional information later. If you lose the report key, it can't be recovered and the case can't be identified or retrieved on your behalf.

### Procedure

1. Navigate to **All > Self-Service > Employee Center**.
2. Access the Anonymous Report Center using **Direct portal access** (`[your instance URL]/arc`) or **Employee center access** (same options as in the table above).
   - If you can't access the center, confirm AI Case Management is installed/active (via an admin) and that pop-up blockers or browser extensions aren't preventing the portal from loading.
   - After accessing the center, you are **signed out automatically** to keep your identity anonymous.
3. On the Anonymous Reporting Center landing page, select **Follow up on a report**.
4. Enter the **Report key**, **Report number**, and the **Text verification** characters.
   > **Note:** If you can't find the Report key and Report number, submit a new case.
5. Select **Next**. This opens your report, which hosts the case status and the history of your interactions with the investigator. Your identity remains confidential throughout.

### What to do next

Use the report to add new information or to respond to investigator comments.
