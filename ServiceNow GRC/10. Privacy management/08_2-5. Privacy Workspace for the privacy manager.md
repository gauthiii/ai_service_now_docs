Creation of information object categories
Information object categories provide a systematic way to classify information objects, making
it easier to align with regulatory requirements. For instance, regulators often group attributes
like iris scans and fingerprints under the category of biometric data. Similarly, email addresses
and phone numbers are commonly categorized as contact information. While regulators focus
on these broad classifications, business users typically refer to the individual data elements
rather than the overarching category. In such scenarios, information object categories serve as a
framework to organize and classify these data elements into their respective broader categories.
Also, if the regulators identify a new data element for a certain category, then the privacy
administrator simply needs to add the data element to the data category and all the assessments
are refreshed. This ability helps save time and you do not have to manually add the new
information objects to the assessments. For information on how to configure information object
categories, see Configure information object categories.
You can create information objects manually. See Create an information object.
Privacy Workspace for the privacy manager
The Privacy management dashboard provides a centralized interface for monitoring and
managing privacy-related data processing activities within the organization.
The visual dashboard provides insights into ongoing processing activities. Privacy managers
and compliance officers can identify potential compliance risks. Data privacy initiatives can be
managed more effectively.
The privacy manager develops and maintains company policies and regulations. A privacy
manager has the sn_privacy.manager role. Privacy managers do the following:
•Manage and monitor the overall organization-level privacy risk and compliance posture.​
The risk and compliance posture represents the overall status of an organization’s risk and
compliance. It spans across business processes, business applications, and related areas.
Assessment is based on risk scores and the compliance status of controls. Evaluation includes
both compliant and non-compliant controls across various regulations.
•Develop and implement privacy regulations, authority documents, policies, and privacy risk
statements.
•Review privacy regulatory requirements and policies.
•Design and monitor risks and controls to deal with breaches of privacy regulations and internal
policies.​
•Plan privacy programs and scope entities.
•Create privacy impact assessment templates.​
•Perform privacy risk assessments to understand the criticality of the processing activities.
•Continuously monitor control effectiveness and suggest effective improvements.

Processing activity tab
The Processing activity tab on the Privacy management dashboard provides a comprehensive
and real-time overview of all data processing activities across the organization.
The Processing activity tab on the Privacy management dashboard supports privacy teams,
compliance officers, and data protection professionals. This dashboard consolidates key
metrics and visualizations to track the status, criticality, compliance levels, and categorization of
processing activities.
By offering intuitive insights through charts, graphs, and filters, the dashboard enables proactive
management of data privacy obligations and facilitates informed decision-making in line with
regulatory requirements. You can use filters and drop-down lists in the widgets to customize the
views based on your preferences.
This dashboard displays the following widgets.
Processing activities by state
This section displays a summary of processing activities categorized by their
current workflow states: New, Discover, Review, Monitor, and Retire. Each state
block provides a quick overview of volume and enables direct navigation to filtered
activity lists.
Processing activities by criticality score
The gauge chart displays the total number of active processing activities
segmented by criticality score: High and Low. This chart enables stakeholders to
prioritize efforts based on regulatory risk classification levels.
If any criticality factor under Regulatory details is marked Yes and criticality is
assessed, the processing activity’s criticality score is set to High. If all factors
are answered as No, then the score is Low. The score is determined using the
Automated criticality factors RAM and can be configured according to requirements.

the data.

Processing activities by data processing role

Processor. Clarity about the role played in processing activities is vital for assigning
responsibilities and meeting legal requirements.

Risk and compliance tab
The Risk and compliance tab on the privacy management dashboard provides a centralized view
of privacy-related risk exposure and regulatory compliance performance.
The Risk and compliance tab on the privacy management dashboard enables organizations to
monitor the risk and compliance postures of the privacy program within the organization. It helps
evaluate how effective current privacy controls are in mitigating identified risks and supporting
compliance.
Using this dashboard, teams can track adherence to major regulatory frameworks, including
NIST SP 800-53 and the EU GDPR. The dashboard presents data through intuitive visualizations
such as heatmaps, compliance scores, and summaries of control objectives that need attention.
These visuals provide immediate insights into risk exposure and compliance gaps across the
organization. Privacy teams can identify high-risk areas and assign priority to remediation tasks
based on real-time data.
The dashboard also assists in confirming continuous regulatory alignment as requirements
evolve, or new risks emerge. By consolidating risk and compliance insights into one view, it
supports faster decision-making and improved accountability across privacy functions.
The visualization and data-driven layout support informed decision-making for privacy teams,
confirming adherence to industry standards and legal obligations. This dashboard displays the
following widgets.

view compliance score.

with status-based color coding.
The Impact assessment widget shows ongoing Impact Assessments related
to Regulatory Assessments. The drop-down menu enables you to change the
assessment category.
Note: These widgets are available only when you have the Regulatory

Privacy cases tab
The Privacy cases tab on the privacy management dashboard serves as a centralized interface
for monitoring, analyzing, and managing privacy-related incidents and requests within an
organization.
The Privacy cases tab on the privacy management dashboard helps organizations achieve and
maintain compliance with data protection regulations. It supports adherence to laws such as the
General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). It also includes the California Consumer Privacy Act
(CCPA) and other global privacy frameworks.
The dashboard empowers privacy managers, analysts & compliance teams, to manage cases
efficiently. It helps quickly identify critical cases needing immediate attention. The dashboard
also reveals emerging trends and recurring issues over time. It uses historical data analysis and
graphical visualizations for deeper insights. You can evaluate cases using multiple criteria like
severity and priority. They can also review the breach status and root cause for each case. This
view helps enable effective resource allocation and timely risk mitigation.
By consolidating case data into an intuitive and interactive interface, the Privacy Cases
Dashboard enhances operational efficiency. It supports data-driven decision-making. It fosters a
proactive, rather than reactive, approach to privacy incident management. It helps to ensure that
all privacy-related inquiries, complaints, or breaches are addressed in accordance with internal
policies and external regulatory requirements.
This dashboard displays the following widgets.
Needs attention
This top-level summary section highlights cases that require immediate or near-
future attention. It comprises three tiles: Overdue cases, Cases due in 7 days, and
Unassigned cases. You can drill-down into the privacy cases from each card.
Case overview
This section offers a high-level graphical breakdown of existing cases based on
various parameters like by state, by breach status, by priority. You can drill-down
into cases from the chart.
Cases
This section displays cases by subtypes by default. You can filter the cases by
impacted business units or by locations.
Cases by primary cause
This section displays a breakdown of cases based on their root or primary cause.
Opened and closed cases in last 12 months
This widget offers historical context by showing trends in opened and closed
privacy cases over the past year. This graph helps identify patterns and workload
over time.
Issues
This widget displays a pie chart summarizing open issues related to privacy cases.
The pie chart is categorized by severity levels like Green (low), Yellow (medium),
Orange (high), and Red (critical). This section enables teams to monitor the volume
and severity of issues, confirming timely follow-up and resolution. You can drill-
down into cases from the chart.
You can create a processing activity, privacy case, or an issue using the Create button.
Additionally, you can filter the privacy cases assigned to all analysts or me.
