## What Is This?

AI Control Tower Monitoring describes how AI Stewards use the AI Control Tower Workspace, specifically the AI Strategy tab, to continuously oversee the execution of AI strategy. It brings together strategy status, financials, prioritized work, governance elements, and the pipeline of upcoming AI initiatives in one place. This is where execution is aligned with strategy and resource allocation is optimized on an ongoing basis.

---

## What AI Stewards Can Monitor

AI Stewards can access the AI Control Tower Workspace and navigate to the **AI Strategy** tab to monitor:

- The status of their strategies, goals, and targets
- Associated costs and financial performance
- The prioritized work currently in progress
- The pipeline of upcoming AI initiatives

Additionally, AI Stewards gain visibility into all execution-related governance elements, including:

- Risks
- Issues
- Decisions
- Actions
- Change Requests

Work items such as Demands, Projects, Epics, or other planning records can also be optionally associated with a specific AI system. This can be done by opening the relevant work record and using the **Product** field to link it to the appropriate AI system. This association provides traceability between strategic goals and the systems delivering them, while remaining flexible and non-mandatory.

For more information, see the **SPM implementation learning program**.

## How to Use This in Implementation

To monitor AI strategy execution, an AI Steward opens the AI Control Tower Workspace and navigates to the AI Strategy tab, where strategy status, goals/targets, costs and financial performance, in-progress prioritized work, the upcoming initiative pipeline, and governance elements (Risks, Issues, Decisions, Actions, Change Requests — collectively RIDAC) are consolidated. To establish traceability between strategy and delivery, open a relevant work record (Demand, Project, Epic, or other planning record) and set its Product field to the appropriate AI system; this linkage is optional and non-mandatory but enables tracing strategic goals to the systems delivering them. The related Strategic Planning and Scenario Planning workspaces support prioritization (MoSCoW), capacity, and financial/budget simulation for AI work. For deeper setup of the underlying planning capabilities, refer to the SPM implementation learning program.
