# How Residual Risk scoring is calculated?

---

## What Is This?

This subtopic explains how ServiceNow calculates residual risk for AI use cases governed through the AI Control Tower. Residual risk represents the risk remaining after controls are applied, and ServiceNow supports three distinct calculation methods. The Matrix Method is the most common approach and is recommended for new implementations.

---

## Residual Risk Calculation Methods

ServiceNow supports three methods for calculating residual risk. The Matrix Method is the most common approach and is recommended for new implementations.

| Method | How It Works | Your Organization's Usage |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Matrix Method | Combines Inherent Risk (X-axis) with Control Effectiveness (Y-axis) in a predefined matrix to output residual risk rating. | Most common method. High Inherent + Ineffective or Needs Improvement = Critical. High Inherent + Effective = Low. |
| 2. Arithmetic (Subtract or Divide) | Subtract: Inherent minus Control Effectiveness. Divide: Inherent divided by Control Effectiveness. | Not typically used for new implementations. |
| 3. Factor Response Method | Residual scored independently via its own factor responses. Not dependent on inherent and control assessments. | Not used in Release 1. Most flexible for complex risk scenarios. |

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

For new implementations, configure the Matrix Method as it is the recommended and most common approach. Under the Matrix Method, residual risk is derived by combining Inherent Risk (X-axis) with Control Effectiveness (Y-axis) in a predefined matrix:

- High Inherent + Ineffective or Needs Improvement = Critical residual risk.
- High Inherent + Effective = Low residual risk.

Because the Matrix Method depends on Control Effectiveness, ensure control attestations are completed; incomplete attestations reduce effectiveness scores and degrade residual risk accuracy. The Arithmetic method (Subtract or Divide) is not typically used for new implementations, and the Factor Response Method is not used in Release 1 (though it is the most flexible for complex risk scenarios).
