## What Is This?

This subtopic clarifies the relationship between the AI Impact Assessment and the Risk Classification in the ServiceNow AI Risk and Compliance practice. While the impact assessment enables the risk classification, they are two separate questionnaires and produce two distinct results. This is important during governance so implementers understand that impact assessment answers do not automatically determine the risk classification score.

---

## Relationship Between Impact Assessment and Risk Classification

The impact assessment enables the risk classification, but they are two separate questionnaires — they are two separate items. The risk classification is just coming from those Risk factors (either OOB or your own). You can review the impact assessment that has been filled out to inform that risk classification. But it is a subjective measurement.

- Impact Assessment answers do NOT automatically feed into the Risk Classification score
- The AI Risk & Compliance Manager or Business Owner can read Impact Assessment answers as context but manually selects each factor score
- A system can trigger many unfavorable Impact Assessment answers and still score Medium in Risk Classification based on the risk scoring methodology applied

In summary: There are two different results.

- One from the impact assessment — how many control objectives were compliant versus not.
- And the second from the risk classification — low, medium, high risk based upon risk factors.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When governing an AI system, treat the AI Impact Assessment and the Risk Classification as two independent outputs. Use the impact assessment to understand control-objective compliance (how many control objectives were compliant versus not), and use the risk classification to determine the system's low/medium/high risk rating based on risk factors. Implementers and the AI Risk & Compliance Manager or Business Owner should review impact assessment answers as context but must manually assign each risk factor score — there is no automatic propagation. Be aware that a system can have many unfavorable impact assessment answers yet still land at Medium risk classification depending on the risk scoring methodology applied.
