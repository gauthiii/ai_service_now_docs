---

## What Is This?

This subtopic explains how the Control Effectiveness percentage is calculated from attestation results in AICT. The resulting score and rating feed directly into the Residual Risk matrix, linking control attestation outcomes to residual risk accuracy. It establishes the score ranges that map raw attestation pass/fail results to qualitative effectiveness ratings.

---

## Control Effectiveness Scoring

How the Control Effectiveness percentage is calculated from attestation results. Feeds directly into the Residual Risk matrix.

| Score Range | Rating | What It Means |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| 100% | Effective | All control attestations passed |
| 60% to 99% | Needs Improvement | Most attestations passed but some have gaps |
| Below 60% | Ineffective | Majority of attestations failed or not completed |

### Note

If a Business Owner does not complete a control attestation task, that control counts as not attested and reduces the effectiveness score below threshold. This is why overdue attestation escalation (the overdue escalation story) is directly tied to residual risk accuracy.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring control effectiveness scoring, ensure attestation tasks are reliably completed by Business Owners, because incomplete attestations count as "not attested" and pull the effectiveness score below threshold. Because the effectiveness score feeds directly into the Residual Risk matrix (as the Control Effectiveness / Y-axis input to the Matrix Method), accurate residual risk depends on timely attestation completion. Implementers should pair this with overdue attestation escalation to maintain residual risk accuracy.
