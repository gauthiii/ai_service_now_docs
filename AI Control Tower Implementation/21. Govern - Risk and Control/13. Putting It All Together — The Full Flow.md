## What Is This?

This subtopic ties together all of the previously defined governance components — Intake, Impact Assessment, Risk Classification, Control Attestations, and Risk Assessment — into a single end-to-end flow. It shows the sequence in which each component runs, which engine drives it, who acts, and what output is produced. It serves as the consolidated reference for how the full risk-and-control governance process operates from intake through residual risk.

---

## The Complete End-to-End Flow

Now that each component is defined and the disconnects are explained, here is the complete end-to-end flow with all five components in sequence:

| # | Step | Engine | Who | What Happens / Output |
|---|------|--------|-----|------------------------|
| 1 | Intake Form | Service Catalog | Business Owner submits | AI System record created in Draft. AI COE notified. No automation fired yet. |
| 2 | Impact Assessment | Smart Assessment Engine | Business Owner answers after AI Steward clicks Start Review | If-then rules fire. Control objectives attached. Risk statements attached. Individual risk records created. Control attestation tasks generated. NOTE: No risk score produced. |
| 3 | Risk Classification | RAM Engine | Business Owner scores 18 factors | Scripted calculator runs. Inherent risk tier applied to AI System: Low / Medium / High / Unacceptable. NOTE: Not influenced by IA answers. |
| 4 | Control Attestations | Task engine | Business Owner attests to each control | Control Effectiveness % calculated: Effective / Needs Improvement / Ineffective. |
| 5 | Risk Assessment | RAM Engine (different workflow) | System calculates automatically | Residual Risk = Inherent Risk x Control Effectiveness. Per individual risk record. Rolls up to AI System. Output: Critical / High / Medium / Low. |

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

An implementer uses this flow as the master sequence when configuring the end-to-end governance process. Key points to honor during build:

- The five components run in order, but they are driven by different engines (Service Catalog, Smart Assessment Engine, RAM Engine, Task engine, and a separate RAM workflow), so each must be configured independently.
- Step 1 (Intake) creates the AI System record in Draft and notifies the AI COE but fires no automation — automation begins only when the AI Steward clicks Start Review at Step 2.
- The Impact Assessment (Step 2) produces no risk score; risk tiering comes from Risk Classification (Step 3), which scores 18 factors and is not influenced by the Impact Assessment answers.
- Control Effectiveness from Step 4 feeds the residual risk calculation in Step 5, where Residual Risk = Inherent Risk x Control Effectiveness, computed per individual risk record and rolled up to the AI System.
