## What Is This?

This section clarifies three commonly confused governance concepts — Risk Statements, Risk Factors, and the Risk Register — and records the key decisions made about each. It distinguishes what each one is and where it is used, then sets out the characteristics the risk statements your organization authors should have. This grounds the configuration of impact assessments, risk classification, and residual risk reporting in AI Control Tower.

---

## Risk Statements vs Risk Factors

This section clarifies each one and records the key decisions made.

|        | Risk Statement | Risk Factor |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| **What it is** | A pre-configured description of a potential risk — e.g. 'AI systems may be targeted for cyber attacks' | One of your organization's scoring dimensions used to calculate inherent risk — e.g. Level of Autonomy |
| **Where used** | Impact Assessment — attached to AI Systems when IA answers trigger them | Risk Classification — scored by Business Owner for each AI System |

---

## Characteristics of Risk Statements to Author

The risk statements your organization needs to author should be:

- General enough to apply to multiple AI systems — not specific to one use case
- Aligned with authority document citations — your internal AI standards, EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF
- Descriptive of a potential harm or failure mode — e.g. 'AI systems processing personal data may expose individuals to privacy violations if data minimization controls are not in place'

---

## Risk Register

- Risk register – is what gets generated after residual risk is calculated with what risks remain.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring AI Control Tower governance, distinguish these three artifacts: author general-purpose Risk Statements (attached to AI Systems via impact-assessment answer triggers and aligned to authority document citations such as internal AI standards, EU AI Act, and NIST AI RMF); define Risk Factors as the scoring dimensions (e.g. Level of Autonomy) the Business Owner uses to calculate inherent risk during Risk Classification; and treat the Risk Register as a downstream output generated after residual risk is calculated, listing the risks that remain. Keep statements broad and reusable so they apply across multiple AI systems rather than to a single use case.
