## What Is This?

This subtopic clarifies the relationship between the AI Impact Assessment and the Risk Assessment within the AI Risk and Compliance governance flow. It explains that the two are connected indirectly, through risk statements, rather than directly. This helps implementers understand how assessment answers, risk classification, and control attestations feed into the overall risk picture for an AI System.

---

## Connection Between Risk Assessment and Impact Assessment

Yes - indirectly, through risk statements:

- Impact Assessment answers trigger risk statements → individual risk records created
- Risk Classification scores the AI System overall for inherent risk tier (separate process)
- Control Attestations → control effectiveness score

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing AI risk governance, treat the Impact Assessment and Risk Assessment as linked but distinct processes joined by risk statements. Configure Impact Assessment questions (e.g., Privacy and Data Protection, Non-Discrimination and Fairness, Transparency and Accountability) so that specific answers trigger risk statements, which in turn generate individual risk records. Run Risk Classification separately to assign the AI System an overall inherent risk tier (e.g., High with a numeric score). Use Control Attestations to derive the control effectiveness score. Together these feed the AI System's overall compliance and risk posture (such as compliance score and risk classification shown on the AI asset record).
