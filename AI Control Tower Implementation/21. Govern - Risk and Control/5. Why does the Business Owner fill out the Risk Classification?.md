# Why does the Business Owner fill out the Risk Classification?

---

## What Is This?

This subtopic explains who is responsible for completing the Risk Classification for an AI System and why the Business Owner is increasingly being asked to do it directly. It clarifies the distinction between Risk Statements and Risk Factors, which are two different concepts that participate in AI governance scoring. It fits into the Govern - Risk and Control workflow where AI Systems are scored for inherent and residual risk.

---

## Who Completes the Risk Classification

Risk Classification is typically completed by the AI COE or AI Risk & Compliance team using the impact assessment answers as context. However, organizations are increasingly asking the Business Owner to fill it out directly since they have the most context about their system. Either approach is valid — the key is that whoever scores the factors has sufficient knowledge of the system to score accurately.

---

## Risk Statements vs Risk Factors — What Is the Difference?

| | Risk Statement | Risk Factor |
|---|---|---|
| What it is | A description of a risk — e.g. 'AI systems may be targeted for cyber attacks' | A scoring dimension — e.g. Level of Autonomy, Data Sensitivity |
| Where used | Impact Assessment — attached when IA answer triggers it | Risk Classification — scored for each AI System |
| Output | Individual risk record. Feeds Risk Assessment (residual risk). | Component of inherent risk tier. Feeds Risk Classification score. |

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing, decide which party will own Risk Classification scoring — either the AI COE / AI Risk & Compliance team (using impact assessment answers as context) or the Business Owner (who holds the most context about their system). Both approaches are acceptable; the prerequisite is that the scorer has sufficient knowledge of the system to score accurately. Keep the conceptual separation clear: Risk Statements are descriptions of risks attached via the Impact Assessment that feed individual risk records and the residual Risk Assessment, while Risk Factors are scoring dimensions (such as Level of Autonomy and Data Sensitivity) scored per AI System that feed the inherent risk tier and the Risk Classification score.
