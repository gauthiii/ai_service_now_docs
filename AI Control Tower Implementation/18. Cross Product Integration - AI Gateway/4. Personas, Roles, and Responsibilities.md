## What Is This?

This subtopic defines the personas involved in operating AI Gateway and the handoffs between them. AI Gateway requires active collaboration across three roles: the AI Steward who governs, the AI Agent Admin who configures, and the AI CoE that sets the policy framework both operate within. Understanding the responsibilities and handoffs between personas is as important as understanding the product itself.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | System Role | Key Responsibilities |
|------|-------------|----------------------|
| AI Steward | sn_ai_governance.ai_steward | Reviews and approves MCP Servers using Asset Approval Playbooks; configures enforcement, pause controls, and PII filtering; monitors observability metrics and responds to incidents; collaborates with AI CoE to ensure decisions reflect defined policy. |
| AI Agent Admin (AI Product Owner) | sn_aia.admin | Creates AI agents and adds MCP Server connections in AI Agent Studio; submits MCP Server requests for AI Steward review; updates agent configurations to route traffic through AI Gateway after approval using Gateway credentials (Client ID, Client secret, Gateway URL). |
| AI CoE | Strategic ownership (no system role) | Defines governance standards — which MCP Servers are acceptable and under what conditions; sets risk and compliance criteria for approvals; determines enforcement activation scope; establishes intake strategy and ownership boundaries. Without this layer, AI Steward decisions are inconsistent and ungovernable at scale. |

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing AI Gateway, assign the `sn_ai_governance.ai_steward` role to those responsible for reviewing and approving MCP Servers and configuring enforcement, pause controls, and PII filtering. Assign `sn_aia.admin` to AI Agent Admins who build agents and route their traffic through AI Gateway using Gateway credentials (Client ID, Client secret, Gateway URL). The AI CoE has no system role but provides strategic ownership — establishing the policy framework, risk/compliance criteria, and intake strategy that make AI Steward decisions consistent and governable at scale. The handoff flow runs from AI Agent Admin (submits MCP Server requests) to AI Steward (reviews/approves) within standards set by the AI CoE.
