## What Is This?

AI Gateway is positioned within the Security/CISO domain of the ServiceNow AI governance stack. For Risk and Compliance teams, it addresses three audit requirements that are difficult to satisfy with direct agent-to-server connections. It enforces a governance framework rather than defining one, relying on the standards set by the AI CoE and consistently applied by AI Stewards.

---

## Audit Requirements Addressed by AI Gateway

| Audit Requirement | How AI Gateway Addresses It |
|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| Inventory | A complete, current registry of every MCP Server connection in use across the enterprise — maintained automatically through AI Control Tower's CMDB integration. |
| Approval Audit Trail | Every MCP Server has a documented approval record with the AI Steward's decision, assessment notes, and full lifecycle history. |
| Runtime Evidence | Observability metrics provide transaction-level evidence of agent activity for audit and incident response purposes. |

## Governance Dependency

AI Gateway enforces a governance framework; it does not define one. The quality of governance depends on the standards the AI CoE establishes and the consistency with which AI Stewards apply them. Activating AI Gateway without a defined policy framework produces a documented but inconsistently governed inventory.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing AI Gateway, position it within the Security/CISO domain to give Risk and Compliance teams the inventory, approval audit trail, and runtime evidence they need. Rely on AI Control Tower's CMDB integration to maintain the MCP Server connection registry automatically, capture AI Steward approval decisions and assessment notes for each MCP Server, and use observability metrics for runtime evidence. Crucially, define a policy framework through the AI CoE before activation — activating AI Gateway without one yields a documented but inconsistently governed inventory.
