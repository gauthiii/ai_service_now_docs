## What Is This?

This is the step-by-step procedure for implementing controls during the Build and Test phase of the AI asset lifecycle. It covers creating Control Attestations, performing attestations (via Classic Survey or Smart Assessment), reviewing attestation results, and completing the remaining Development Plan and Pre-deployment lifecycle tasks. The procedure is driven by Lifecycle Tasks assigned to the AI Risk and Compliance Analyst and the AI Product Owner, and is exercised from the AI Control Tower Workspace, AI Risk and Compliance Workspace, Risk Portal, and Employee Portal.

---

## A. Create Control Attestation

During the Build & Test phase, a Lifecycle Task called "Create Control Attestation" is assigned to the AI Risk and Compliance Analyst. Their role is to scope relevant controls and initiate the Attestation Request, essentially reminding them to create Control Attestations.

Steps and access paths:

- Work on assigned request by AI Risk & Compliance Manager from AI Risk & Compliance Workspace > Tasks.
- Monitor assigned request by AI Risk & Compliance Manager from Risk Portal > Tasks.
- Work on assigned request for control attestation by AI Risk & Compliance Manager.
- Work on assigned request for control attestation > Scope Key Controls by AI Risk & Compliance Manager.
- Complete creation of Control Attestation by AI Risk & Compliance Manager.

## How to Use This in Implementation

To implement controls during Build and Test, complete the lifecycle tasks in sequence: the AI Risk & Compliance Analyst first completes "Create control attestation" by scoping the relevant key controls on the AI asset (Controls related list, "Add controls from control objective"). This triggers "Perform control attestation," assigned to the AI Product Owner/Submitter, who responds either via Classic Survey (answering "Is the control implemented?" with an Explain note) or via Smart Assessment, accessible from the Risk Portal, AI Control Tower Workspace, or Employee Portal. To speed completion, select multiple controls with similar responses and use the Group assessments / Combine action to provide one response for all grouped attestations. The AI Risk & Compliance Manager then reviews the attestation responses (state moves through Draft → Assigned → Work in progress → Review → Closed) from their group's tasks. Remaining Development Plan tasks (deployment-region info, value templates) are demonstration reminders, and the Pre-deployment phase requires completing the Conformity assessment (e.g., the EU AI Act impact questionnaire) before the asset can move toward deployment. Note that the deployment-region and value-template lifecycle tasks are stated to be for demonstration purposes only.
