## What Is This?

Regulatory Agencies are external regulatory, supervisory, or standard-setting bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems. Within AI Control Tower governance, this subtopic establishes a centralized library of agencies so implementers can identify the relevant authorities responsible for overseeing industries or sectors within each jurisdiction. It fits into the Govern – Risk and Controls area as the foundation for linking AI compliance obligations to the bodies that enforce them.

---

## Overview

Agencies are external regulatory, supervisory, or standard-setting bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems. A Regulatory Agency is an independent agency responsible for exercising laws or standards or guidelines in a specific field or activity or business operations.

---

## Key Value Benefits

It establishes a centralized library of agencies for identifying relevant regulatory authorities that are responsible for overseeing industries or sectors within each jurisdiction.

- European Commission
- National financial regulators overseeing AI credit decisions

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers begin by populating the centralized agency library: add each Regulatory Agency that oversees the relevant jurisdictions, industries, or sectors (for example, the European Commission or national financial regulators overseeing AI credit decisions). Next, relate Authority Documents to their issuing agency — in the Authority Document form, set the "Regulated By" field to establish the relationship with the Agency. No additional configuration is required. Note the gotcha that the 360-degree view association is not supported for these relationships.
