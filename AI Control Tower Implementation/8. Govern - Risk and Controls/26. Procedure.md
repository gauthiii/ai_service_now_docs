## What Is This?

This is the Procedure for working with Regulatory Agencies in the Govern - Risk and Controls capability of AI Control Tower. Regulatory Agencies are external regulatory, supervisory, or standard-setting bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems. The procedure defines the steps an implementer follows to register an agency and link it to the authority documents it governs.

---

## Procedure for Regulatory Agencies

The procedure consists of the following steps:

A. Add a Regulatory Agency (link)
B. Relate Authority Document (link)

### Note

- In Authority Document, use the form field "Regulated By" to build a relation with the Agency.
- 360-degree view association is not supported.

### Related Context (Regulatory Agencies)

- **Overview:** Agencies are external regulatory, supervisory, or standard-setting bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems. A Regulatory Agency is an independent agency responsible for exercising laws or standards or guidelines in a specific field or activity or business operations.
- **Key Value Benefits:** It establishes a centralized library of agencies for identifying relevant regulatory authorities that are responsible for overseeing industries or sectors within each jurisdiction. Examples include:
  - European Commission
  - National financial regulators overseeing AI credit decisions
- **Configuration:** Not Applicable

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

To register a regulatory authority in AI Control Tower, an implementer first adds the Regulatory Agency record, then relates the relevant Authority Document(s) to it. The relationship between an Authority Document and its Agency is established from the Authority Document side using the "Regulated By" form field. Note that no additional configuration is required for this capability, and a 360-degree view association between the agency and its documents is not supported.
