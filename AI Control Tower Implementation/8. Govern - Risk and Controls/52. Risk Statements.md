## What Is This?

Risk Statements describe a specific AI-related risk in a structured format, typically including cause, event, and impact. They are part of a pre-defined risk library that is configured before any AI systems go through the workflow. The AI Risk Statement Library in ServiceNow AI Risk and Compliance provides a centralized, structured collection of pre-defined AI risk statements that organizations can use to identify, assess, and manage AI risks consistently across the enterprise.

## Overview

Risk Statements describe a specific AI-related risk in a structured format, typically including cause, event, and impact. The AI Risk Statement Library in ServiceNow AI Risk and Compliance provides a centralized, structured collection of pre-defined AI risk statements that organizations can use to identify, assess, and manage AI risks consistently across the enterprise.

| Aspect | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **What it is** | A pre-defined description of a risk that may apply to an AI system. It is part of a risk library that is configured before any AI systems go through the workflow. |
| What it does | Describes a risk that needs to be monitored and managed. When attached to an AI System via the Impact Assessment questions and answers, it creates an individual risk record for that specific system. |
| Who does it | Pre-configured by Platform Admin. Sourced from your organization's authority documents and risk library. Attached to AI Systems automatically via Impact Assessment answers. |
| Output | Individual risk records created on the AI System. These feed the Risk Assessment (residual risk) process later. |

## How to Use This in Implementation

As a Platform Admin, pre-configure the Risk Statement Library before AI systems go through the workflow, sourcing statements from your organization's authority documents and risk library. Each Risk Statement should be structured to capture the cause, event, and impact of an AI-related risk. Once configured, Risk Statements attach automatically to AI Systems via the Impact Assessment questions and answers, generating an individual risk record for each specific system. These individual risk records subsequently feed the Risk Assessment (residual risk) process. Use the "Create Risk Statement" procedure to author new entries in the library.
