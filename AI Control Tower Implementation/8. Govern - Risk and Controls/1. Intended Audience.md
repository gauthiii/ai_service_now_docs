## What Is This?

This subtopic identifies who the AI Risk & Compliance Implementation Guide is written for. It defines the roles expected to read, apply, and act on the guide as they configure and operationalize an AI Risk & Compliance solution that supports effective AI governance. It sits at the start of the Govern - Risk and Controls section, framing the responsibilities of each audience across the AI lifecycle.

---

## Intended Audience

The intended audience for the AI Risk & Compliance Implementation Guide would be:

- **Implementation Engineers / Solution Integrators** – responsible for installing and configuring the solution
- **Compliance & Risk Technology Specialists** – bridging technical setup with governance and regulatory requirements
- **AI Stewards** – overseeing solution deployment in alignment with organizational AI governance strategy

### Section Context

The broader guide provides implementers with a structured, step-by-step approach to configure and operationalize an AI Risk & Compliance solution that supports effective AI governance. It ensures technical teams can deploy the solution in alignment with regulatory requirements, organizational policies, and best practices, enabling consistent risk management, compliance monitoring, and audit readiness across the AI lifecycle.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

Use this section to confirm role alignment before starting the implementation. Implementation Engineers / Solution Integrators handle the hands-on install and configuration; Compliance & Risk Technology Specialists ensure the technical setup maps to governance and regulatory requirements; and AI Stewards provide oversight to keep the deployment aligned with the organization's AI governance strategy. Ensuring these roles are staffed and coordinated is a prerequisite for delivering consistent risk management, compliance monitoring, and audit readiness across the AI lifecycle.
