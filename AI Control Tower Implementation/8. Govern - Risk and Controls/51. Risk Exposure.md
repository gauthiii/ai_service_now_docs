## What Is This?

Risk Exposure describes how risks are defined and grouped within the Govern stage of the AI Control Tower using the Risk Framework and Risk Statements tables. It establishes the structure for categorizing and detailing risks so that stakeholders can analyze AI-related exposure. With Advanced Risk enabled, it supports hierarchical risk structures that break high-level risks into more specific sub-risks.

---

## Risk Exposure

The Risk Framework and Risk Statements tables are used to define and group various risks. While Risk Frameworks serve more as high-level categories or containers, Risk Statements describe specific risks. With Advanced Risk enabled, organizations can create hierarchical risk structures, meaning one risk can be broken down into several sub-risks, allowing for more detailed and structured risk analysis. For example, an "Information Technology Risk" might include sub-risks like IT operations, infrastructure, and performance, helping stakeholders drill down into precise risk areas.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

To implement Risk Exposure, use the Risk Framework table to define high-level risk categories or containers, and the Risk Statements table to describe the specific risks within them. Enable Advanced Risk to build hierarchical risk structures, decomposing a broad risk (e.g., "Information Technology Risk") into granular sub-risks (such as IT operations, infrastructure, and performance) for more detailed and structured analysis. This grouping enables stakeholders to drill down into precise risk areas during AI governance.
