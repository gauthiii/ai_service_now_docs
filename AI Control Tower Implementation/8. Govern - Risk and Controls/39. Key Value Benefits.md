## What Is This?

This subtopic describes the Key Value Benefits of Control Objectives and Controls within the AI Control Tower's govern, risk, and controls capability. It explains why these constructs matter: they form the foundation for responsible and ethical AI usage and operationalize governance and compliance requirements. It fits into implementation as the rationale that justifies defining Control Objectives and the Controls that satisfy them.

---

## Key Value Benefits — Control Objectives

Foundation for Responsible & Ethical AI Usage. They provide a governance bridge between regulatory requirements and operational controls.

Example: "Ensure AI systems are monitored for discriminatory outcomes throughout their lifecycle."

---

## Key Value Benefits — Controls

They operationalize governance and compliance requirements. Examples include below:

- Mandatory bias testing before model deployment
- Human review required for AI-assisted decisions

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When defining Control Objectives, implementers should treat them as the governance bridge linking regulatory requirements (such as the EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF) to the concrete Controls that enforce them, framing each objective as a measurable outcome (e.g., monitoring AI systems for discriminatory outcomes). Controls are then created to operationalize these objectives — for example, requiring mandatory bias testing before model deployment and human review for AI-assisted decisions. Together they ensure responsible and ethical AI usage while keeping governance and compliance requirements actionable.
