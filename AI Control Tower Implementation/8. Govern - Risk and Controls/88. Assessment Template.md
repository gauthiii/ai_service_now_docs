## What Is This?

The Assessment Template capability in the AI Control Tower provides a suite of assessment tools used to evaluate AI use cases and AI Assets for risk and regulatory conformity. It includes the AI Impact Assessment, which identifies potential risks such as copyright issues, bias, privacy breaches, misinformation, and surveillance, as well as AI Impact Assessments designed specifically for EU AI Act conformity. These assessments are triggered at specific stages of the AI Asset onboarding and deployment lifecycle.

---

## Overview

The AI Control Tower offers a robust suite of assessment tools, including the AI Impact Assessment, which evaluates AI use cases to identify potential risks such as copyright issues, bias, privacy breaches, misinformation, and surveillance. Additionally, the AI Control Tower provides AI Impact Assessments specifically designed for EU AI Act conformity.

The AI Impact assessment determines the mappings of the Risks and Controls to the AI Assets. These are outlined in the risk mapping section below. This is triggered during the assess stage of the onboarding process of the AI Asset.

The AI Impact Assessment for Conformity is to ensure that high-risk AI systems do not pose harm to users, society, or the environment, and meet regulatory obligations as per the AI Act. This is triggered via the playbook during the pre-deployment checks.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing the Assessment Template, plan for two distinct trigger points: the AI Impact Assessment runs during the "assess" stage of the AI Asset onboarding process and is responsible for mapping Risks and Controls to the AI Assets (per the risk mapping section). The AI Impact Assessment for Conformity is triggered via the playbook during pre-deployment checks to validate that high-risk AI systems meet EU AI Act regulatory obligations and do not cause harm to users, society, or the environment. Use the standard AI Impact Assessment for general risk identification (copyright, bias, privacy, misinformation, surveillance) and the conformity-specific assessment for EU AI Act compliance.
