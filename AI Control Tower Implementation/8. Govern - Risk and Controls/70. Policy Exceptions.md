## What Is This?

Policy Exceptions are formally approved deviations from established policies or controls within the AICT Govern – Risk and Controls process. They provide an authorized, time-bound mechanism to operate outside a policy when a justified business need arises, while preserving governance oversight. They fit into implementation as the controlled escape valve that lets teams proceed despite a gap, managed through a defined lifecycle.

---

## Overview

Policy Exceptions are formally approved deviations from established policies or controls. A policy exception allows for a formal, authorized deviation from established policies, typically for a specific duration and user. These exceptions are granted when a justified business need arises, and they are managed through a lifecycle with defined stages, including analysis, review, and approval.

---

## Key Value Benefits

They allow controlled flexibility while maintaining governance oversight. Examples include below:

1. Use of Non-Approved Training Data – AI model trained on datasets not cleared under the organization's data governance policy.
2. Bypassing Model Validation Steps – Deployment without completing required fairness or robustness testing.
3. Third-Party AI Service Without Review – Integrating an external AI API without security and compliance clearance.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When a justified business need requires operating outside an established policy or control, an implementer raises a policy exception rather than silently violating governance. Scope the exception to a specific duration and user, and route it through the defined lifecycle stages (analysis, review, and approval) so the deviation remains controlled and auditable. Use the "Request for Policy Exception" procedure to initiate the request. Typical triggers include using non-approved training data, bypassing model validation steps, or integrating a third-party AI service without review — in each case the exception preserves governance oversight while permitting necessary flexibility.
