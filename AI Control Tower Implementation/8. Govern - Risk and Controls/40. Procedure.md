## What Is This?

This subtopic describes the procedure for working with Control Objectives in the AI Control Tower. Control Objectives describe what outcome a set of controls is intended to achieve, without prescribing how. The procedure covers creating a Control Objective and mapping it to a citation so that governance policies can be traced back to authority documents.

---

## Procedure Steps

The Procedure for Control Objectives consists of the following steps:

A. Create a Control Objective (link)
B. Map a Control Objective to a Citation (link)

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

An implementer first creates a Control Objective to define a governance outcome (a statement describing something the organization must or should do to govern an AI system, which can be actively measured and tested against). They then map that Control Objective to a citation, linking it to authority documents such as the organization's internal AI standards, the EU AI Act, or the NIST AI RMF. This mapping provides traceability and supports automated monitoring and validation of policy adherence. Control Objectives are pre-configured by the Platform Admin during implementation and are attached to AI Systems automatically via Impact Assessment answers.
