## What Is This?

Controls are specific policies, procedures, or technical measures designed to mitigate risks and meet control objectives. In the AI Control Tower, they operationalize governance and compliance requirements, translating high-level control objectives into concrete, enforceable measures applied to AI systems.

---

## Overview

Controls are specific policies, procedures, or technical measures designed to mitigate risks and meet control objectives.

---

## Key Value Benefits

They operationalize governance and compliance requirements. Examples include below:

- Mandatory bias testing before model deployment
- Human review required for AI-assisted decisions

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers use Controls to put control objectives into practice as enforceable policies, procedures, or technical measures that mitigate identified risks. When defining controls, ground them in concrete, operational requirements (for example, mandatory bias testing before model deployment or required human review for AI-assisted decisions) so that governance and compliance expectations are actively enforced rather than only documented.
