## What Is This?

Citations are the specific, actionable requirements or clauses contained within an authority document. They define what an organization must comply with, breaking down a broad authority document into discrete obligations. In implementation, citations are mapped to controls so that compliance can be tracked and evidenced against concrete regulatory requirements.

## How to Use This in Implementation

To apply citations, first ensure the relevant authority document exists, then create individual citations that capture each actionable requirement or clause (for example, an EU AI Act article on human oversight or an ISO 42001 clause on AI risk assessment). After creating a citation, map it to the appropriate control(s) using Citation to Control Mapping so that compliance obligations are linked to the controls that satisfy them. Refer to the linked Create a Citation and Citation to Control Mapping resources, and the referenced Community Post, for the detailed steps.
