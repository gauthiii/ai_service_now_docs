## What Is This?

This Overview introduces the Risk Assessment Methodology (RAM) used within the Govern – Risk and Controls practice. A RAM is a configured object that defines the types of risk assessments to perform and the entities on which those assessments are run. It establishes the foundation for both regulatory risk classification and risk assessment in your organization.

---

## Risk Assessment Methodology (RAM)

The Risk Assessment Methodology (RAM) is used for assessing either the risks or objects in your organization. A RAM is configured to specify the types of risk assessments and the entities on which risk assessment is performed. A configured RAM is an object with associated assessment types that have associated factors.

### About Regulatory Risk Classification (using Object-based RAM)

| Aspect | Description |
| --- | --- |
| What it is | A questionnaire scored against your organization's pre-configured risk factors to calculate an inherent risk tier for the AI System as a whole. |
| What it does | Determines HOW RISKY the AI system is overall. Primary factors set the base tier. Control factors can escalate the tier. Produces: Low / Medium / High / Unacceptable. |
| Who does it | Business Owner fills it out. The task can be reassigned to R&C Manager if needed. |
| Output | Inherent risk tier applied to AI System record: Low / Medium / High / Unacceptable. |

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing the Govern – Risk and Controls practice, configure a RAM as the object that defines which assessment types and entities are evaluated. Use the Object-based RAM to drive Regulatory Risk Classification: a Business Owner (or reassigned R&C Manager) completes a questionnaire scored against pre-configured risk factors, where primary factors set the base tier and control factors can escalate it, ultimately applying an inherent risk tier (Low / Medium / High / Unacceptable) to the AI System record. The Risk-based RAM supports the broader Risk Assessment process detailed in subsequent sections.
