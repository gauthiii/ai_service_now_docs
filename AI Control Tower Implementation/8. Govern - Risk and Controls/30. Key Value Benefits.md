## What Is This?

This subtopic describes the "Key Value Benefits" delivered by two governance constructs in AI Control Tower: Regulatory Agencies and Authority Documents. These benefits explain why each construct matters when establishing risk and controls governance for AI systems. They sit within the Govern – Risk and Controls area, helping implementers map AI systems to the external authorities and source texts that drive compliance obligations.

---

## Key Value Benefits — Regulatory Agencies

Regulatory Agencies are external regulatory, supervisory, or standard-setting bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems. A Regulatory Agency is an independent agency responsible for exercising laws or standards or guidelines in a specific field or activity or business operations.

**Key value benefit:** It establishes a centralized library of agencies for identifying relevant regulatory authorities that are responsible for overseeing industries or sectors within each jurisdiction.

Examples:

- European Commission
- National financial regulators overseeing AI credit decisions

---

## Key Value Benefits — Authority Documents

Authority Documents are official regulatory texts, standards, or guidance issued by an agency.

**Key value benefit:** They serve as the source of compliance obligations.

Examples:

- EU Artificial Intelligence Act
- ISO/IEC 42001 AI Management System
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing Govern – Risk and Controls, use Regulatory Agencies to build a centralized library of the external authorities relevant to your AI systems' jurisdictions and sectors (for example, the European Commission or national financial regulators). Use Authority Documents to capture the official texts (for example, the EU Artificial Intelligence Act, ISO/IEC 42001, or the NIST AI Risk Management Framework) that act as the source of your compliance obligations. The two constructs are intended to be related to each other so that each Authority Document is tied back to the Agency that issued it, providing traceability from compliance obligations to the issuing regulatory body.
