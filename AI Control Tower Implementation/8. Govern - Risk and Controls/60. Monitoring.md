## What Is This?

Monitoring is the part of the Govern - Risk and Controls workflow that provides ongoing oversight of risk exposure and control performance over time. It is implemented through Indicators, which are metrics that collect data to monitor controls and risks and to gather audit evidence. This subtopic explains how Indicators are used to track a single control or risk and to support the monitoring, mitigation, and reporting of risks.

---

## Indicators

### Overview

Indicators are metrics used to monitor risk exposure or control performance over time. Indicators collect data to monitor controls and risks, and to collect audit evidence. Indicators monitor a single control or risk. They are used to enhance and facilitate the monitoring, mitigation, and reporting of risks.

- **Control Indicators:** These are metrics or measures used to assess the effectiveness of controls. They can be created at any stage of the control lifecycle.
- **Draft Stage:** While a control is in the Draft stage, it's typically not finalized or implemented. However, ServiceNow allows creating control indicators during this stage.
- **Monitor Stage:** Controls in the Monitor stage are actively implemented and ready for assessment. This stage is the most logical time to start using control indicators and generating indicator tasks.

### Key Value Benefits

They support ongoing oversight and early warning.

## How to Use This in Implementation

To implement monitoring, configure Control Indicators against the controls and risks you need to track. Although ServiceNow permits creating control indicators while a control is still in the Draft stage, the most logical point to begin using them and to generate indicator tasks is once the control reaches the Monitor stage, where it is actively implemented and ready for assessment. Follow the "Create Control Indicator" procedure to define each metric, then use "Manage Control Indicator" for ongoing collection of monitoring data and audit evidence. Each indicator monitors a single control or risk, so plan one indicator per item you intend to oversee, enabling early warning and continuous reporting of risk exposure.
