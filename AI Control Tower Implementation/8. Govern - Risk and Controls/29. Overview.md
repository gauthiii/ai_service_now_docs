## What Is This?

This overview introduces two foundational governance entities in the Govern - Risk and Controls area: Regulatory Agencies and Authority Documents. Regulatory Agencies are the external bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems, and Authority Documents are the official texts those agencies publish. Together they establish the centralized library of regulators and the compliance obligations that govern AI use cases within each jurisdiction.

---

## Regulatory Agencies — Overview

Agencies are external regulatory, supervisory, or standard-setting bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems. A Regulatory Agency is an independent agency responsible for exercising laws or standards or guidelines in a specific field or activity or business operations.

### Key Value Benefits

It establishes a centralized library of agencies for identifying relevant regulatory authorities that are responsible for overseeing industries or sectors within each jurisdiction.

- European Commission
- National financial regulators overseeing AI credit decisions

## Authority Documents — Overview

Authority Documents are official regulatory texts, standards, or guidance issued by an agency.

### Key Value Benefits

They serve as the source of compliance obligations.

- EU Artificial Intelligence Act
- ISO/IEC 42001 AI Management System
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers use the Regulatory Agencies library to register the external bodies (for example, the European Commission or national financial regulators) that oversee AI systems in each relevant jurisdiction, then add the Authority Documents (such as the EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001, or the NIST AI RMF) those agencies issue as the source of compliance obligations. To link the two, populate the "Regulated By" form field on an Authority Document to build the relation back to its Agency. Note that no separate configuration is required for Regulatory Agencies, and the 360-degree view association is not supported for these records.
