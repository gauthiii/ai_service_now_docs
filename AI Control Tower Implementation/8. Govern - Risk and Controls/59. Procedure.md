## What Is This?

Within the Govern - Risk and Controls area, "Procedure" refers to the action links provided for creating the core governance artifacts described on the page: Risk Statements and Risks. These procedures point the implementer to the create flows used to instantiate risk library entries (Risk Statements) and actively managed risk instances (Risks) in ServiceNow AI Risk and Compliance.

---

## Procedure — Risk Statements

The Risk Statement section provides the following procedure:

- **Create Risk Statement (link)**

For context, Risk Statements describe a specific AI-related risk in a structured format, typically including cause, event, and impact. The AI Risk Statement Library in ServiceNow AI Risk and Compliance provides a centralized, structured collection of pre-defined AI risk statements that organizations can use to identify, assess, and manage AI risks consistently across the enterprise. A Risk Statement is a pre-defined description of a risk that may apply to an AI system, part of a risk library configured before any AI systems go through the workflow. When attached to an AI System via the Impact Assessment questions and answers, it creates an individual risk record for that specific system. The output is individual risk records created on the AI System, which feed the Risk Assessment (residual risk) process later.

---

## Procedure — Risks

The Risks section provides the following procedure:

- **Create Risk (link)**

For context, Risks are recorded, assessed risk instances that are actively managed. They enable prioritization, treatment, and monitoring. Example: Bias risk associated with an automated credit approval model used in multiple jurisdictions.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

An implementer applies these procedures by following the "Create Risk Statement" link to pre-configure the risk library (typically done by a Platform Admin, sourced from the organization's authority documents and risk library) before any AI systems go through the workflow. Risk Statements attach automatically to AI Systems via Impact Assessment answers, generating individual risk records. The implementer then follows the "Create Risk" link to record and manage actual risk instances, which support prioritization, treatment, and monitoring and feed into the residual Risk Assessment process.
