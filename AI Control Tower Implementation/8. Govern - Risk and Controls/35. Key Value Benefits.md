## What Is This?

This subtopic describes the key value benefits of Citations within the Govern - Risk and Controls practice. Citations are the specific, actionable requirements or clauses within an authority document, and their value is that they define what an organization must comply with. This sits within the broader governance flow that connects authority documents, citations, and controls.

---

## Key Value Benefits of Citations

They define what must be complied with.

- EU AI Act Article requiring human oversight for high-risk AI systems
- ISO 42001 clause on AI risk assessment

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing governance for AI, use Citations to capture the specific, actionable requirements or clauses drawn from authority documents (for example, an EU AI Act Article or an ISO 42001 clause). Each citation defines a concrete compliance obligation that can subsequently be mapped to controls. Implementers create citations and then perform Citation-to-Control mapping so that compliance requirements are tied to the controls that satisfy them.
