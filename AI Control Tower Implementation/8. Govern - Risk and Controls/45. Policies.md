## What Is This?

Policies are formal organizational statements that define expectations, principles, and rules for AI use. They are internal to the organization and set the tone and governance standards for AI. Within the Govern – Risk and Controls area, policies provide the foundation from which control objectives are derived and measured.

---

## Overview

Policies are formal organizational statements that define expectations, principles, and rules. Policies are internal to organization. Usually, all the policy statements that requires to be measured in the policy document will be created as control objectives. When creating control objectives under policies, it is AI compliance manager responsibility to map the control objectives to external regulations (specifically, the citations) such as the EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF.

Companies use their own template for defining Policy Documents. A sample policy may contain sections such as Content and Purpose, Definitions, Controls in Policies, Responsibilities, Revisions and References. Among these sections, Controls section in the policy are the one to be considered for control objectives of the policy.

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers should use their organization's own policy template to define Policy Documents, ensuring a Controls section is present since those controls feed the policy's control objectives. To apply this: create the policy, then publish it. The AI compliance manager is responsible for mapping the control objectives created under policies to external regulatory citations such as the EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF. Any policy statement that must be measured should be captured as a control objective. No additional configuration is required.
