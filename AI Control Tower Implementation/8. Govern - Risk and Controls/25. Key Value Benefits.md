## What Is This?

This subtopic describes the "Key Value Benefits" delivered by two governance constructs in the AI Control Tower: Regulatory Agencies and Authority Documents. It explains the practical value each provides for managing AI regulatory compliance, and gives concrete examples of agencies and authority documents. It fits within the Govern - Risk and Controls area, where AI systems are mapped to the external bodies and texts that impose compliance obligations.

---

## Key Value Benefits — Regulatory Agencies

Regulatory Agencies are external regulatory, supervisory, or standard-setting bodies that issue requirements applicable to AI systems. A Regulatory Agency is an independent agency responsible for exercising laws or standards or guidelines in a specific field or activity or business operations.

It establishes a centralized library of agencies for identifying relevant regulatory authorities that are responsible for overseeing industries or sectors within each jurisdiction.

Examples:

- European Commission
- National financial regulators overseeing AI credit decisions

## Key Value Benefits — Authority Documents

Authority Documents are official regulatory texts, standards, or guidance issued by an agency. They serve as the source of compliance obligations.

Examples:

- EU Artificial Intelligence Act
- ISO/IEC 42001 AI Management System
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing the Govern - Risk and Controls area, use Regulatory Agencies to build a centralized library of the external authorities (such as the European Commission or national financial regulators) that oversee the industries or jurisdictions relevant to your AI systems. Use Authority Documents to capture the official regulatory texts and standards (such as the EU Artificial Intelligence Act, ISO/IEC 42001, and the NIST AI Risk Management Framework) that serve as the source of compliance obligations. To connect the two, open the Authority Document form and set the "Regulated By" field to relate it to the appropriate Agency. Note that no dedicated configuration is required for Regulatory Agencies, and the 360-degree view association is not supported.
