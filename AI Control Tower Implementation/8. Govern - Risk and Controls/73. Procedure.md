## What Is This?

This subtopic provides the procedural entry point for handling Policy Exceptions within the AICT governance process. Policy Exceptions are formally approved deviations from established policies or controls, granted when a justified business need arises and managed through a lifecycle with defined stages. The Procedure points the implementer to the action used to formally request such an exception.

---

## Procedure

Request for Policy Exception (link)

The procedure for managing a Policy Exception begins with submitting a **Request for Policy Exception**, which is provided as a link in the source material.

This procedure sits within the broader Policy Exception process, where:

- A policy exception allows for a formal, authorized deviation from established policies, typically for a specific duration and user.
- Exceptions are granted when a justified business need arises.
- They are managed through a lifecycle with defined stages, including **analysis**, **review**, and **approval**.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

To apply this in implementation, use the **Request for Policy Exception** action (the provided link) to initiate the formal request whenever a justified business need requires deviating from an established policy or control. The request then proceeds through the defined lifecycle stages — analysis, review, and approval — so that controlled flexibility is maintained alongside governance oversight. No additional configuration steps are described on this page beyond invoking the request action.
