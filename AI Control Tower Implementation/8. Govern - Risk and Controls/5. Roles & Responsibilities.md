## What Is This?

This subtopic establishes a common role model for the AI Risk & Compliance solution that aligns governance, risk, compliance, technical, and business stakeholders. It defines who is accountable across installation, configuration, governance, and ongoing compliance monitoring. It fits into the Govern - Risk and Controls phase of implementation, ensuring critical governance activities are not delayed by unclear ownership.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this section is to establish a common role model that aligns governance, risk, compliance, technical, and business stakeholders. AI governance spans multiple disciplines—legal, compliance, risk management, data science, IT, and business leadership—and without clearly defined responsibilities, critical governance activities such as risk assessments, approvals, monitoring, and issue remediation may be delayed or inconsistently applied.

The roles and responsibilities outline the key stakeholders involved in implementing and operating the AI Risk & Compliance solution, defining their accountabilities across installation, configuration, governance, and ongoing compliance monitoring. Clear role assignment ensures effective collaboration, regulatory alignment, and sustained operational ownership of the solution.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing the Govern - Risk and Controls capabilities, use this role model as the baseline for assigning accountability. Identify stakeholders across the spanning disciplines (legal, compliance, risk management, data science, IT, and business leadership) and map them to the system-defined roles. Assign responsibilities across the four areas — installation, configuration, governance, and ongoing compliance monitoring — to avoid delays in risk assessments, approvals, monitoring, and issue remediation. Refer to the linked system-defined roles to confirm the exact roles available out of the box before assigning users.
