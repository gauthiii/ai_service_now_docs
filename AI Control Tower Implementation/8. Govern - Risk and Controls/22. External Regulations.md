## What Is This?

External Regulations are the rules and laws created by governments and regulators that control how artificial intelligence can be used. Within the Govern - Risk and Controls area of AI Control Tower, they establish the legal and compliance boundaries that AI systems, use cases, and models must operate within. Their main goal is to make sure AI systems are safe, fair, transparent, and do not cause harm to people or organizations.

---

## Definition and Purpose

External regulations are rules and laws created by governments and regulators that control how artificial intelligence can be used. Their main goal is to make sure AI systems are safe, fair, transparent, and do not cause harm to people or organizations.

These regulations explain what organizations are allowed to do, what they must do, and what they must avoid when designing, using, or deploying AI. They often place extra requirements on AI systems that can significantly affect people's lives, such as systems used for hiring, lending, healthcare, or public services.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When governing AI within AI Control Tower, implementers should map applicable external regulations to the AI systems and use cases being governed—paying particular attention to high-impact domains such as hiring, lending, healthcare, and public services, where additional regulatory requirements commonly apply. Use these regulations to define what the organization is allowed to do, what it must do, and what it must avoid when designing, using, or deploying AI, ensuring that controls support safety, fairness, transparency, and harm avoidance.
