## What Is This?

This subtopic summarizes the value that Control Objectives and Controls deliver within the AI Control Tower's Govern (Risk and Controls) capability. It explains why these governance constructs matter, connecting regulatory requirements to operational measures. It fits in implementation as the rationale for configuring Control Objectives and Controls to enforce responsible AI usage.

---

## Key Value Benefits — Control Objectives

Foundation for Responsible & Ethical AI Usage. They provide a governance bridge between regulatory requirements and operational controls.

Example: "Ensure AI systems are monitored for discriminatory outcomes throughout their lifecycle."

---

## Key Value Benefits — Controls

They operationalize governance and compliance requirements. Examples include below:

- Mandatory bias testing before model deployment
- Human review required for AI-assisted decisions

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring Control Objectives, treat them as the bridge between authority documents (internal AI standards, EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF) and the operational controls that enforce them — defining what outcomes the organization must achieve. Use Controls to translate those objectives into concrete, enforceable measures (for example, mandatory bias testing before model deployment or required human review for AI-assisted decisions). Together these establish a foundation for responsible and ethical AI usage that can be measured and tested against.
