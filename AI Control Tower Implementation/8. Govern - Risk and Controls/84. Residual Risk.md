---

## What Is This?

Residual Risk is the final calculation that shows how much risk remains AFTER controls have been attested. It combines the Inherent Risk score with the Control Effectiveness score to determine whether an AI system is still at risk once controls are in place. It is calculated automatically by the system and rolls up from individual risk records to the AI System level.

---

## Residual Risk Overview

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| What it is | The final calculation showing how much risk remains AFTER controls have been attested. Combines Inherent Risk score with Control Effectiveness score. |
| What it does | Produces a residual risk score per individual risk record. Shows whether the AI system is still at risk after controls are in place. Rolls up to AI System level. |
| Who does it | Calculated automatically by the system. No manual input required. |
| Output | Residual risk per individual risk: Critical / High / Medium / Low. Rolled up to AI System record. |

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

Residual Risk requires no manual configuration or input from an implementer—it is computed automatically once Inherent Risk scores are established and controls have been attested (which feed the Control Effectiveness score). To rely on this metric, ensure inherent risk scoring and control attestation are completed first, since residual risk depends on both inputs. Use the per-risk residual scores (Critical / High / Medium / Low) and the AI System level roll-up to judge whether remaining risk is acceptable after controls are applied.
