## What Is This?

This subtopic explains how lifecycle governance and risk/compliance execution are handled through distinct but interconnected records within the AI Control Tower architecture. It identifies the two core records — the AI Asset Governance Details record and the AI System record — and clarifies the separation of concerns between AI Control Tower (AICT) and AI Risk and Compliance (GRC). It fits into the Govern - Lifecycle phase of implementation, where AI Stewards oversee assets across lifecycle phases.

---

## Lifecycle Object Alignment

Within the AI Control Tower architecture, lifecycle governance and risk/compliance execution are handled through distinct but interconnected records.

### The AI Asset Governance Details record

`[sn_ai_governance_asset_governance_details]`

Serves as the primary lifecycle governance record within AI Control Tower. It provides AI Stewards with:

- End-to-end lifecycle visibility
- Governance state tracking
- Oversight across all lifecycle phases

### The AI System record

`[sn_grc_ai_gov_ai_system]`

Is the operational record leveraged by AI Risk and Compliance (GRC) to:

- Execute risk assessments
- Manage compliance workflows
- Track regulatory alignment

### Separation of Concerns

Together, these records ensure separation of concerns:

- **AICT:** Lifecycle governance and orchestration
- **GRC:** Risk, compliance, and control execution

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing lifecycle governance, recognize that two distinct records back the architecture: the AI Asset Governance Details record (`sn_ai_governance_asset_governance_details`) is owned by AI Control Tower for lifecycle visibility, governance state tracking, and oversight, while the AI System record (`sn_grc_ai_gov_ai_system`) is owned by GRC for risk assessments, compliance workflows, and regulatory alignment. Implementers should maintain the separation of concerns — using AICT for lifecycle governance and orchestration and GRC for risk, compliance, and control execution — so that the two interconnected records reinforce each other without overlapping responsibilities.
