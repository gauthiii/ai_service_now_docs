## What Is This?

This subtopic is the second of the two core components of the Playbook Architecture (following the Automation Layer). It describes how customers can extend the AI Asset Lifecycle playbook through Flow Designer, and the underlying workflow design principles that keep those extensions upgrade-safe. It fits into the Govern - Lifecycle area as guidance for safely customizing lifecycle automation.

---

## Customization and Extensibility

Customers can extend the playbook through Flow Designer. Following is the underlying workflow design:

- **Clone, don't modify** — Clone existing Flows and Subflows and adjust triggers and conditions to support additional AI asset types. Avoid deactivating OOB tasks or modifying them directly to preserve upgrade compatibility.
- **Task customization** — Define new lifecycle tasks using Flow Designer conditions and adjust sequencing as needed.
- **Lifecycle stage order** — Maintain the standard Assess → Build & Test → Deploy sequence.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When extending the AI Asset Lifecycle playbook, implementers should clone existing Flows and Subflows rather than editing the out-of-the-box (OOB) versions, adjusting triggers and conditions on the clones to handle additional AI asset types. Define any new lifecycle tasks via Flow Designer conditions and adjust their sequencing, while keeping the standard Assess → Build & Test → Deploy stage order intact. Avoid deactivating or directly modifying OOB tasks to preserve upgrade compatibility.
