## AI Asset Lifecycle Overview

The AI asset lifecycle defines the series of phases required to manage any AI asset — including AI systems, models, datasets, and prompts — from initial intake through retirement. Each phase has defined activities, responsible personas, and governance checkpoints to ensure responsible development, deployment, and ongoing oversight.

### Phase 1: Intake / Onboard — Primary Persona: AI Product Owner

**Intake / New / Onboard**

Primary Persona: AI Product Owners

AI Product Owners initiate the AI asset lifecycle by submitting AI use case requests through the Employee Center. These requests capture key details related to the AI models, datasets, and intended business use.

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers use this overview as the conceptual map for configuring AI asset governance in AI Control Tower. Establish the AI Asset Repository and ensure AI assets (systems, models, datasets, prompts) can be onboarded via Employee Center use case requests. Configure the lifecycle phases — Demand, Build & Validate, and Deploy/Monitor/Assess Value — and assign the responsible personas (AI Product Owner, AI Steward, AI Risk & Compliance Manager, and supporting governance teams) to their respective activities and governance checkpoints. Note that the lifecycle relies on understanding Asset State, Lifecycle Phase, and Lifecycle Status, and that some tasks are expected to be configured as part of the GOTB playbook based on each customer's specific AI governance process. Phase 1 (Intake/Onboard) begins with AI Product Owners submitting requests, so the intake form should capture model, dataset, and intended business use details.
