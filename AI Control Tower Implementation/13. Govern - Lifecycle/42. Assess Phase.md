# Assess Phase

> ServiceNow AI Control Tower (AICT) — Implementation Guide
> Section: Govern - Lifecycle → Assess Phase
> Source: AI-Control-Tower.pdf, p.180-184

---

## What Is This?

The Assess Phase is the first of the Lifecycle Flows by Phase that execute automatically as an AI System asset progresses through the governance lifecycle. It is triggered when the AI Governance Details record transitions to the Assess phase, and it creates the tasks that drive impact assessment, governance reviews (legal, security, architecture), analyst assignment, and risk assessment. These flows and subflows generate the governance and compliance activities required before an AI System can move toward development.

---

## Lifecycle Flows by Phase (Context)

The following flows and subflows execute automatically as an AI System asset progresses through the lifecycle. Each flow is triggered by a specific lifecycle status change and creates the tasks that drive governance and compliance activities.

---

## Flow: AIRC Flow — AI Governance Details Enters Assess

**Trigger:** AI Governance Details record transitions to Assess phase

| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Flow initiation | Triggers when AI Governance Details enters Assess phase |
| 1.1 | Transition to Assess phase | Trigger point: AI Governance Details record moves to Assess |
| 1.2 | Create Impact Assessment task | AI asset approval task — type: *Impact Assessment* |
| 1.3 | Assign Impact Assessment task | Task assigned to AI Risk & Compliance Managers |
| 1.4 | Complete and review Impact Assessment | AI Asset Owner completes; AI Risk & Compliance Manager reviews and closes |
| 1.5 | Sync Impact Assessment tasks | AICT and AIRC assessment tasks kept in sync |
| 2 | Create Legal Collaboration task | AI approval task — type: *AI Governance Task* |
| 3 | Create Security Clearance task | AI approval task — type: *AI Governance Task* |
| 4 | Create Architecture Review task | AI approval task — type: *AI Governance Task* |

### Subflows called during Assess

1. **Trigger Smart Assessment** — initiates the Impact Assessment
2. **Map AI Control Objectives to AI System** — upon Impact Assessment completion, when sent for AIRC review; adds controls after review is complete
3. **Map AI Risk Statements to AI System** — upon Impact Assessment completion, when sent for AIRC review; adds risks after review is complete
4. **Change Assessment State** — updates Impact Assessment state
5. **Assign Analyst to AI System** — upon assessment review completion, captures the reviewing analyst on the AI System record
6. **Generate Risk Assessment** — upon assessment completion, generates a risk assessment for the AIRC analyst

> **Note:** From AICT, the Impact Assessment task moves to *In Review*. In AIRC, the AI Impact Assessment progresses to *Review* simultaneously. The AIRC user persona closes the AI Impact Assessment, which marks it as closed in AICT as well.

## Flow: Generate Risk Assessment

**Trigger:** AI System record is updated

| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Flow initiation | Triggers when AI System record is updated |
| 1.1 | Create Perform Risk Assessment task | AI approval task — type: *Risk Assessment* |
| 1.2 | Create object-based Risk Assessment | Generated for the AI System and assigned to an AI Risk & Compliance user |
| 2 | Complete Risk Assessment | Task completed by AI Risk & Compliance user |
| 3 | Sync Risk Assessments | AI approval task and object-based assessment in AICT and AIRC kept in sync |

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring the Assess phase, ensure that the AI Governance Details record's transition into Assess is the trigger that initiates the AIRC Flow. The flow automatically creates the Impact Assessment task (assigned to AI Risk & Compliance Managers, completed by the AI Asset Owner) along with Legal Collaboration, Security Clearance, and Architecture Review governance tasks. Verify that AICT and AIRC assessment tasks remain in sync, since closing the AI Impact Assessment in AIRC also marks it closed in AICT. The downstream "Assign Analyst to AI System" flow (triggered when the Impact Assessment closes) populates the Risk Analyst field with the closing user, and the "Generate Risk Assessment" flow (triggered when the AI System record updates) produces the object-based risk assessment for the AIRC analyst. The supporting subflows — Trigger Smart Assessment, Map AI Control Objectives, Map AI Risk Statements, Change Assessment State, Assign Analyst, and Generate Risk Assessment — must be available so controls and risks are mapped to the AI System after AIRC review completes.
