## What Is This?

The AI Asset Lifecycle defines the governance and management process for AI assets within an organization. It divides the process into distinct stages that ensure responsible development, deployment, and ongoing oversight of any AI asset type — including AI systems, models, datasets, and prompts — from initial onboarding through retirement. It is the foundational framework that AI Control Tower uses to govern AI assets across their full lifespan.

## AI Asset Lifecycle Overview

The AI asset lifecycle defines the series of phases required to manage any AI asset — including AI systems, models, datasets, and prompts — from initial intake through retirement. Each phase has defined activities, responsible personas, and governance checkpoints to ensure responsible development, deployment, and ongoing oversight.

---

## Phase 1: Intake / Onboard — Primary Persona: AI Product Owner

### Intake / New / Onboard

**Primary Persona: AI Product Owners**

AI Product Owners initiate the AI asset lifecycle by submitting AI use case requests through the Employee Center. These requests capture key details related to the AI models, datasets, and intended business use.

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers use the AI Asset Lifecycle as the master governance workflow that AI Control Tower orchestrates. To apply it:

- Map your organization's AI assets (systems, models, datasets, prompts) into the **AI Asset Repository**.
- Align activities to the three high-level phases — **Demand**, **Build & Validate**, and **Deploy, Monitor & Assess Value** — and assign the responsible personas (AI Product Owner, AI Steward, AI Risk & Compliance Manager, and other AI governance teams).
- Begin with **Phase 1: Intake / Onboard**, where AI Product Owners submit AI use case requests via the Employee Center capturing models, datasets, and intended business use.
- Note the gotcha highlighted in the figure: assessment tasks shown in the flow are intended to be configured as part of the GOTB playbook by customers, based on their own AI governance process — they are not fixed out of the box.
