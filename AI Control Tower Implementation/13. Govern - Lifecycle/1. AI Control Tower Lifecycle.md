## What Is This?

AI Control Tower Lifecycle describes how ServiceNow AI Control Tower governs AI assets end-to-end — from initial onboarding through retirement. Built on the ServiceNow AI Platform, it gives AI Centers of Excellence (CoEs) and Chief AI Officers (CAIOs) centralized oversight of AI systems, models, datasets, prompts, and their inputs and outputs. It positions AI Control Tower as a single intelligence hub for managing, governing, and optimizing AI operations at scale.

---

## Overview

ServiceNow AI Control Tower is engineered to empower AI Center of Excellence and Chief AI Officers (CAIOs) in overseeing organizational AI operations. Built on the ServiceNow AI Platform, it delivers comprehensive tools for the management, governance, and optimization of AI systems, models, datasets, prompts, and its inputs and outputs.

ServiceNow AI Control Tower is built on the ServiceNow® AI Platform and designed to empower AI Centers of Excellence (CoEs) and Chief AI Officers (CAIOs) with the tools they need to manage, govern, and optimize AI operations at scale. The platform provides centralized oversight of AI models, datasets, prompts, and their inputs and outputs — across both cloud-based and on-premises environments.

Regardless of how AI solutions are sourced — whether developed internally, acquired from third-party vendors, or embedded within SaaS platforms — AI Control Tower serves as a single intelligence hub that governs AI assets from initial onboarding through retirement. A native AI asset inventory within the Configuration Management Database (CMDB) anchors this hub, delivering business context by linking AI initiatives directly to enterprise services and assets — something few standalone governance tools can offer.

This foundation supports end-to-end workflows that span the full AI lifecycle, enabling seamless collaboration across strategy, IT, risk, legal, and business teams. The result is holistic AI management — not siloed oversight — with every stakeholder working from a shared system of record.

To support this, AI Control Tower provides specialized workspaces tailored to every role within the AI CoE — including AI stewards, product owners, and risk and compliance managers — enabling robust asset tracking and effective lifecycle and risk management.

## AI Lifecycle States

The AI Risk and Compliance Workspace tracks AI systems across the stages of their lifecycle. The lifecycle states shown are:

| Lifecycle State | Description |
| --- | --- |
| New | Newly registered AI systems not yet progressed. |
| Assess | AI systems under assessment. |
| Build | AI systems in the build stage. |
| Review for deployment | AI systems being reviewed prior to deployment. |
| Live and Monitor | AI systems that are live and being monitored. |
| Offboard | AI systems being retired/offboarded. |

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers use AI Control Tower as the central system of record for governing all AI assets across their lifecycle. Begin by ensuring AI assets (systems, models, datasets, prompts) are inventoried in the CMDB so they carry business context and link to enterprise services. Register AI systems and progress them through the lifecycle states (New → Assess → Build → Review for deployment → Live and Monitor → Offboard), and use the AI Risk and Compliance Workspace dashboards to monitor systems by state and by department. Provision the role-specific workspaces for AI stewards, product owners, and risk and compliance managers, and track ongoing risk and AI assessments to maintain governance across both cloud-based and on-premises environments.
