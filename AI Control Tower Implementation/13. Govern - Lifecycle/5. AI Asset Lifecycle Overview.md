## AI Asset Lifecycle Overview

The AI asset lifecycle defines the series of phases required to manage any AI asset — including AI systems, models, datasets, and prompts — from initial intake through retirement. Each phase has defined activities, responsible personas, and governance checkpoints to ensure responsible development, deployment, and ongoing oversight.

### Diagram/Screenshot: Figure 1. AI Asset High-level Lifecycle

The diagram presents a high-level, swimlane-style lifecycle flow spanning three top-level phase columns:

- **Demand**
- **Build & Validate**
- **Deploy, Monitor & Assess Value**

The left side lists the personas (rows / swimlanes) involved:

- **AI Risk & Compliance Manager**
- **AI Steward**
- **Product Owner**
- **Other AI governance teams (security, legal, data, governance, etc.)**

The flow reads roughly left to right:

- In the **Demand** phase, the Product Owner submits a request for an AI asset (new system/model/dataset/prompt) via ServiceNow AI Agent Studio, which feeds into the **AI Asset Repository** (shown as a "now" datastore).
- The AI Steward maps the request to an AI asset and Product Owner responds to assessments; assigned/scheduled assessment tasks flow to the AI Risk & Compliance Manager.
- In the **Build & Validate** phase, activities include the Risk Manager responding to risk classification/risk assessment, "Close complete," analysis of lifecycle data, monitoring compliance of tasks, and Regulatory Risk Classification. Risks and controls are identified for the AI asset. Collaboration occurs with multiple stakeholders (legal, security, etc.).
- In the **Deploy, Monitor & Assess Value** phase, steps shown include Controls representation and risk mitigation, Issue management and policy exception, Continuous control monitoring, and Track value and adoption of the AI asset.
- A decision/hexagon node labeled around "Employee logs an AI need / issue" branches to an incident-style flow: **Triage and investigate → Resolve**, and **Post case review → Close**.

A footnote marker (star) indicates: *Tasks to be configured as part of the GOTB playbook by customers based on their AI governance process.*

The diagram uses arrows connecting boxes/icons to show the progression of activities and handoffs between personas across the three phases.

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers use this overview to understand the end-to-end governance flow before configuring AI Control Tower. Each lifecycle phase (Demand; Build & Validate; Deploy, Monitor & Assess Value) has defined activities, responsible personas (AI Product Owner, AI Steward, AI Risk & Compliance Manager, and other governance teams), and governance checkpoints. Per the diagram footnote, certain tasks are configured as part of the GOTB playbook by customers based on their own AI governance process. Begin with Phase 1 (Intake / Onboard), where AI Product Owners submit AI use case requests through the Employee Center to capture details about AI models, datasets, and intended business use, which populate the AI Asset Repository for downstream risk classification, assessment, monitoring, and value tracking.
