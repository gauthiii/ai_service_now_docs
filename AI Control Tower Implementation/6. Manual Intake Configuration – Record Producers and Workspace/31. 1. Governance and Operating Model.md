## What Is This?

This is the first of the "Implementation Considerations – AI Asset Inventory (AICT)" items. It defines the governance and operating model an organization should establish to manage AI assets across their lifecycle. It focuses on ownership, accountability (RACI), approval gates, regulatory alignment, and risk-tier classification within the AI Control Tower.

---

## Governance and Operating Model

- Establish a clear ownership model:
  - AI Asset Owner → lifecycle accuracy
- AI Steward / CoE → governance enforcement
  - Risk & Compliance → oversight and controls
- Define RACI across lifecycle stages (Intake → Review → Build → Deploy → Monitor → Retire)
- Implement approval gates (e.g., Steward review before AI System activation)
- Align governance with regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001)
- Classify AI systems by risk tier (low / medium / high) and apply differentiated controls

### Diagram/Screenshot: Lifecycle Stages Flow

The RACI definition references a linear lifecycle flow across six stages:

```
Intake → Review → Build → Deploy → Monitor → Retire
```

Each stage is a point at which roles (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) are assigned, with approval gates such as a Steward review occurring before an AI System is activated.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

To apply this in implementation: first assign ownership roles — AI Asset Owner (responsible for lifecycle accuracy), AI Steward / Center of Excellence (governance enforcement), and Risk & Compliance (oversight and controls). Build a RACI matrix mapped to the six lifecycle stages (Intake, Review, Build, Deploy, Monitor, Retire), and insert approval gates such as a mandatory Steward review prior to activating an AI System. Ensure the governance model aligns with the EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, and ISO 42001, and classify each AI system into a low/medium/high risk tier so that differentiated controls can be applied accordingly.
