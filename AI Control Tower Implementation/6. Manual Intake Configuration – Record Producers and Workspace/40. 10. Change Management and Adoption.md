## What Is This?

This subtopic covers the change management and adoption practices that accompany manual intake configuration for AI assets in AICT. It focuses on governing how AI assets are onboarded, training users, monitoring for non-compliant behavior, and continuously improving intake processes. The goal is to position AICT as the authoritative system of record for all AI assets across the organization.

---

## Change Management and Adoption Practices

- Establish intake governance policies across business units
- Train users on:
  - Asset onboarding processes
  - Governance expectations
- Monitor and address:
  - Shadow AI usage
  - Non-compliant asset creation
- Iterate on processes based on:
  - Adoption maturity
  - Feedback loops
- Position AICT as the system of record for all AI assets

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

After configuring manual intake (record producers and workspace), an implementer should formalize intake governance policies for each business unit and train end users on both the asset onboarding workflow and the governance expectations tied to it. Set up monitoring to detect shadow AI usage and non-compliant asset creation, and use adoption-maturity signals and feedback loops to iteratively refine the intake processes. Reinforce AICT as the single system of record so all AI assets are captured and governed consistently.
