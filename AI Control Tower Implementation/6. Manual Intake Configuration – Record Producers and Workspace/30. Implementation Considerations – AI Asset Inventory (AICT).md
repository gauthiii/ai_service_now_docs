## What Is This?

This subtopic captures the practical implementation considerations for building and operating an AI Asset Inventory within the AI Control Tower. It outlines how organizations should govern, scope, and model their AI assets so the inventory remains accurate, complete, and aligned to regulatory frameworks. It sits at the end of the manual intake configuration work, framing the operating discipline needed once intake mechanisms (record producers and workspace) are in place.

---

## Implementation Considerations

### 1. Governance and Operating Model

- Establish a clear ownership model:
  - AI Asset Owner → lifecycle accuracy
- AI Steward / CoE → governance enforcement
  - Risk & Compliance → oversight and controls
- Define RACI across lifecycle stages (Intake → Review → Build → Deploy → Monitor → Retire)
- Implement approval gates (e.g., Steward review before AI System activation)
- Align governance with regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001)
- Classify AI systems by risk tier (low / medium / high) and apply differentiated controls

### 2. Inventory Scope and Strategy

- Define what qualifies as an AI Asset (ML, GenAI, Agentic, embedded AI)
- Standardize classification of AI use cases as AI Systems
- Determine scope across:
  - Business applications
  - Third-party tools
  - Shadow/unsanctioned AI usage
- Continuously refine scope as AI adoption evolves
- Ensure inclusion of agentic architectures and multi-model systems

## How to Use This in Implementation

After configuring manual intake (record producers and workspace), apply these considerations to make the AI Asset Inventory operational and trustworthy. Begin by assigning ownership roles (AI Asset Owner, AI Steward / CoE, Risk & Compliance) and codifying a RACI across the full lifecycle (Intake → Review → Build → Deploy → Monitor → Retire), with approval gates such as a Steward review before activating an AI System. Define the inventory scope to cover business applications, third-party tools, and shadow/unsanctioned AI, including agentic and multi-model systems, and classify assets by risk tier (low / medium / high) to apply differentiated controls aligned to the EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, and ISO 42001.
