## What Is This?

This subtopic is one of the implementation considerations for the AI Asset Inventory in AI Control Tower (AICT). It defines how an organization should scope and strategize what gets tracked in the AI asset inventory — clarifying what qualifies as an AI Asset, how AI use cases are classified, and how the inventory scope evolves as AI adoption grows. It sits within the broader "Implementation Considerations – AI Asset Inventory (AICT)" guidance.

---

## Inventory Scope and Strategy

- Define what qualifies as an AI Asset (ML, GenAI, Agentic, embedded AI)
- Standardize classification of AI use cases as AI Systems
- Determine scope across:
  - Business applications
  - Third-party tools
  - Shadow/unsanctioned AI usage
- Continuously refine scope as AI adoption evolves
- Ensure inclusion of agentic architectures and multi-model systems

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When building the AI asset inventory in AICT, first establish a clear definition of what counts as an AI Asset, covering machine learning, generative AI, agentic AI, and embedded AI. Standardize how AI use cases are classified as AI Systems so the inventory remains consistent. Set the inventory scope to span business applications, third-party tools, and shadow/unsanctioned AI usage so coverage is complete. Treat scope as an ongoing activity — refine it as AI adoption matures, and make sure agentic architectures and multi-model systems are explicitly included rather than overlooked.
