## What Is This?

This subtopic is part of the AICT Customization Guide and explains how to customize the AI asset lifecycle by adding, modifying, or deleting lifecycle phases. It covers the recommendations and the step-by-step procedure for adding a new lifecycle phase to the `sn_ai_governance_lifecycle` table and wiring it into the corresponding playbook in Workflow Studio. It fits into implementation when an organization needs to extend the out-of-box (OOB) governance lifecycle to match their own process.

---

## Recommendations (Rough Notes)

- Deletion of an OOB phase is not recommended at all.
- It is ideal to not update the order of the OOB phases. I.e. do not place Assess after Build. For more information on editing existing phases please refer to the "Playbook: AI Asset Lifecycle" section.
- To add new lifecycle phases, an AI steward can add more entries to the `sn_ai_governance_lifecycle` table, then update the playbook.

## Steps to Add, Modify, or Delete Lifecycle Phases

If a customer wants to add, modify or delete lifecycle phases, they can follow the below steps:

1. Add a new lifecycle phase to AI Asset Lifecycles (`sn_ai_governance_lifecycle`) table.
2. Navigate to flow designer > Playbooks.
3. Find the relevant playbook. Click on the Add icon in the required position and fill the form. E.g.

## How to Use This in Implementation

To extend the AI asset lifecycle, an AI steward first creates a new record in the `sn_ai_governance_lifecycle` (AI Asset Lifecycles) table with the desired lifecycle phase (e.g., `Risk Evaluation`) and domain. They then open the relevant playbook in Flow Designer > Playbooks, insert a new stage at the correct position using the Add icon (configuring its start rule relative to existing stages), and add an activity from the AI Asset Management activity set. The activity is configured (label, activity definition, automation flow, assignment group, and entry/current lifecycle phase) to align with the new phase. Key gotchas: do not delete OOB phases, and do not reorder OOB phases (e.g., do not place Assess after Build); refer to the "Playbook: AI Asset Lifecycle" section before editing existing phases.
