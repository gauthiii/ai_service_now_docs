## What Is This?

The Procedure for "Assess – Evaluate AI Use Case Impacts" describes the operational lifecycle flows that run once an AI asset is managed for risk and compliance evaluation. It walks through performing the Impact Assessment, reviewing the assessment, performing the Risk Assessment, and the supporting lifecycle collaboration tasks (architecture review, security clearance, legal collaboration). It fits in the Assess phase of AI asset onboarding, where AI Product Owners and AI Risk & Compliance Managers respond to and review assessments.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

An implementer follows these lifecycle flows after an AI asset enters the managed/Assess state. The AI Product Owner responds to the Impact Assessment (accessible from AI Control Tower Workspace Tasks, Risk Portal, or directly on the AI asset), completing the questionnaire across sections such as Privacy and Data Protection and Non-Discrimination and Fairness, then submitting. The AI Risk & Compliance Manager reviews the response (via Operations or the AI Assessments list), previews the resulting Control Objectives and Risk Statements, and marks Update State as "Closed complete" to advance evaluation. Separately, the AI Steward triggers the Risk Assessment (Perform risk assessment task), which the AI Risk & Compliance Manager completes using the "Risk classification for AI system" methodology to produce a regulatory risk classification score. Supporting lifecycle collaboration tasks (architecture review, security clearance, legal collaboration) are reminder tasks assigned to the AI Product Owner for cross-functional review and are demonstration-only. For how outcomes (control objectives/risk statements) are mapped, refer to the Post Assessment Automation Rule topic.
