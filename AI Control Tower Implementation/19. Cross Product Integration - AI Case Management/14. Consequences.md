## What Is This?

Consequences are the effects or outcomes that result from an identified cause within an AI-related incident, inquiry, or compliance concern. They are a configurable element of AI Case Management, used to capture the operational, regulatory, financial, reputational, or ethical impact of a case. Documenting consequences supports impact assessment and helps prioritize remediation in line with governance and compliance priorities.

---

## Consequences

Consequences are the effects or outcomes that result from an identified cause. These may be operational, regulatory, financial, reputational, or ethical in nature. Documenting consequences supports impact assessment, helps prioritize response efforts, and ensures remediation actions align with governance and compliance priorities.

Common examples include:

- Incorrect or biased AI decisions affecting customers
- Regulatory non-compliance leading to fines or sanctions
- Operational delays or service disruptions
- Loss of stakeholder trust
- Ethical breaches impacting public perception

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing AI Case Management, configure Consequences to record the downstream effects of each identified cause. Pair them with Causes (the underlying triggers) so that root-cause analysis connects directly to its outcomes, enabling accurate impact assessment and prioritization of remediation. Use the categories present (operational, regulatory, financial, reputational, ethical) to align documented consequences with governance and compliance priorities. Follow the page's configuration reference for the specific setup steps.
