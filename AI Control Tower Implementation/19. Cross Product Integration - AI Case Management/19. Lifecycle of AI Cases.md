## What Is This?

The Lifecycle of AI Cases describes how AI Risk and Compliance case analysts move an AI-related case through its full set of stages, from initial reporting to closure. It establishes the standard workflow for triaging, investigating, resolving, reviewing, and closing AI Cases, and shows how collaborators across the organization participate. This fits into the broader AI Case Management capability used to manage, track, and resolve inquiries, incidents, or investigations related to AI assets.

---

## The Five Stages of the AI Case Lifecycle

AI Risk and Compliance case analysts manage AI-related cases through five stages: triage, investigation, resolution, post-case review, and closure.

Cases enter the workflow when employees report an AI-related concern through the Employee Center Portal or submit anonymously via the Anonymous Reporting Center. Once received, analysts perform investigations, assess potential breaches, gather supporting evidence, and initiate remediation actions. Cases involving fraud or financial process failures follow dedicated workflows that bring in compliance, risk, and financial analysts as needed. Security breaches follow a parallel process in which security analysts and compliance case analysts collaborate on analysis, containment, eradication, and closure.

Throughout the investigation, the AI Risk and Compliance team reviews reports, assesses risks, and coordinates evidence collection, interviews, and legal counsel where required. Once findings are established, outcomes are documented and corrective actions are determined — these may include training, disciplinary measures, or policy updates. Affected parties are notified of case resolution in a manner that maintains appropriate confidentiality.

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers should configure AI Case workflows to support the five lifecycle stages (triage, investigation, resolution, post-case review, and closure) and ensure intake channels are enabled — the Employee Center Portal and the Anonymous Reporting Center. Set up dedicated workflows for specialized case types (e.g., fraud or financial process failures, and security breaches) so the appropriate analysts (compliance, risk, financial, security) are engaged. Enable collaboration with Finance, Employee, HR, and Legal stakeholders for evidence gathering and resolution planning, and ensure resolution notifications preserve confidentiality. Map cases to impacted areas such as AI Assets, Policies, and Regulations to support cause analysis and remediation tracking.
