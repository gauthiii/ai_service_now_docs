## What Is This?

AI-related inquiries are formal requests for information, clarification, or investigation about AI systems, their decisions, or compliance status. They are a critical component of AI governance, ensuring transparency, accountability, and adherence to regulatory and ethical standards. Within AI Case Management, inquiries provide a documented and trackable mechanism for stakeholders to seek explanations and compliance evidence about AI systems.

## Overview of AI-Related Inquiries

AI-related inquiries are formal requests for information, clarification, or investigation about AI systems, their decisions, or compliance status. They are a critical component of AI governance, ensuring transparency, accountability, and adherence to regulatory and ethical standards.

Inquiries typically originate from:

- AI asset owners
- Business users or employees seeking compliance evidence
- Internal governance teams validating AI practices
- Business stakeholders requesting explanations of AI decisions
- AI system owners providing technical responses

## Submission Channels

Inquiries can be submitted through three channels:

- **Employee Center Portal** — Employees can submit an inquiry by providing relevant details and supporting documents, ensuring timely routing and tracking (the page references "click here" for steps).
- **Anonymous Reporting Center** — Where confidentiality is required, concerns can be submitted anonymously.
- **Dedicated workspaces (manual submission)** — Compliance analysts can submit inquiries manually from dedicated workspaces, enabling them to request information, clarification, or evidence about AI systems in a documented and trackable way (the page references "click here" for steps).

## Mapping and Reporting

Inquiries can be mapped to affected AI assets — including AI systems, models, and datasets — providing reporting insights that support effective decision-making by AI Stewards and the AI Risk and Compliance team.

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers should enable the inquiry intake channels relevant to their organization: the Employee Center Portal for general employee submissions, the Anonymous Reporting Center for confidential/anonymous concerns, and manual submission from dedicated workspaces for compliance analysts. Ensure inquiries can be mapped to the affected AI assets (AI systems, models, and datasets) so that AI Stewards and the AI Risk and Compliance team gain reporting insights for decision-making. Use the Tracking dashboard in the AI Risk and Compliance Workspace to monitor inquiry-driven action tasks and issues by state and overdue status.
