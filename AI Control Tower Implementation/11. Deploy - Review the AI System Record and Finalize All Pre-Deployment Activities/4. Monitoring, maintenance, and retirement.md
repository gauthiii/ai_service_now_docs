## What Is This?

This subtopic describes the post-deployment lifecycle stage of an AI system within the AI Control Tower, where a live AI system is continuously observed, maintained, and ultimately retired. It tracks both technical health (accuracy, model drift) and business outcomes (ROI, adoption), and covers ongoing work such as retraining the model and decommissioning it when it is no longer useful or too costly to run. It fits at the end of the Deploy phase, after the AI system has gone live.

---

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

After deploying the AI system, implementers should establish continuous monitoring using the Control Tower dashboards to track accuracy, model drift, bias, and performance degradation, alongside business outcomes such as ROI and adoption. Define Indicator Templates for AI performance, AI safety, and AI harm category, then map those templates to controls and interpret the AI system output. Set up AI Cases to report AI-related incidents or raise enquiries for clarification from the AI Council. Plan for ongoing maintenance such as model retraining, and retire the system when it is no longer useful or cost-effective.
