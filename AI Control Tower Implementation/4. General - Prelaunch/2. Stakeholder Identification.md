## What Is This?

Stakeholder Identification is the first Foundational-level step in the General Prelaunch checklist for an AICT deployment. It involves identifying the core personas required for the initial AICT deployment before activating any plugins or provisioning roles. It establishes who will own governance, manage AI asset lifecycles, handle risk and compliance, and sponsor the program.

---

## Identify Core Personas Before Deployment

Identify the core personas required for initial AICT deployment before activating any plugins or provisioning roles. Refer to the Personas, Roles, and Responsibilities section of the AICT Implementation Guide for detailed role definitions.

### Core Stakeholders Checklist

| ✓ | Core Stakeholders |
|---|---|
| ☐ | Identify the AI Steward (or equivalent) who will own AICT governance and oversight |
| ☐ | Identify AI Product Owners responsible for managing AI asset lifecycles |
| ☐ | Identify the AI Risk and Compliance Manager (or equivalent) for risk assessments and compliance activities |
| ☐ | Confirm executive sponsor for the AI governance program (e.g., CIO, CAIO, CRO) |

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

This is the first item under the Foundational deployment level and should be completed before plugin activation and core role provisioning. Implementers should map each named persona (AI Steward, AI Product Owners, AI Risk and Compliance Manager, and an executive sponsor such as CIO, CAIO, or CRO) to actual people in the organization, consulting the Personas, Roles, and Responsibilities section of the AICT Implementation Guide for detailed role definitions. As noted in the checklist guidance, roles should be assigned to user groups rather than individual users to simplify ongoing access management and ensure consistent permissions across the AICT deployment.
