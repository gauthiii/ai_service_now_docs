---

## What Is This?

Core Role Provisioning is a prelaunch step that establishes access for the core personas who will use AI Control Tower. It involves creating user groups and assigning the out-of-the-box roles for each persona. Roles must be assigned to groups, not to individual users.

---

## Overview

Create user groups and assign out-of-the-box roles for core personas. Roles must be assigned to groups, not individual users.

---

## Core Roles

| ✓ | Core Roles |
|---|---|
| ☐ | Create a user group for AI Stewards and assign the AI Steward role (sn_ai_governance.ai_steward) |
| ☐ | Assign the AI Control Tower Workspace User role (sn_ai_governance.workspace_user) to the AI Stewards group |
| ☐ | Create a user group for AI Product Owners and assign the AI Asset Owner role (sn_ai_asset_mgmt.ai_asset_owner) |
| ☐ | Create a user group for AI Risk and Compliance and assign the AI Risk and Compliance Manager role (sn_grc_ai_gov.ai_risk_and_compliance_manager) |
| ☐ | Assign the AI Risk and Compliance Admin role (sn_grc_ai_gov.ai_risk_and_compliance_admin) to platform administrators responsible for AICT configuration |
| ☐ | Verify that each persona can access their respective workspace |

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

After plugin activation, an implementer creates a dedicated user group for each core persona (AI Stewards, AI Product Owners, AI Risk and Compliance) and assigns the corresponding out-of-the-box role to the group rather than to individual users. The AI Stewards group additionally requires the AI Control Tower Workspace User role, and platform administrators responsible for AICT configuration receive the AI Risk and Compliance Admin role. Finally, confirm each persona can access their respective workspace before proceeding to workspace verification and inventory population.
