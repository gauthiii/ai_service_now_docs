## What Is This?

Agent Platform Maturity is one of the "Emerging Considerations" — active areas of evolution that implementation teams should monitor and incorporate into governance processes as practices mature. It highlights that many third-party agent platforms are not yet enterprise-ready, which affects how AI agents can be discovered, governed, and managed within the AI Control Tower.

---

## Agent Platform Maturity

Many third-party agent platforms are not yet enterprise-ready. They cannot easily accommodate discovery or support migrating agents through an SDLC process with corporate gate provisions. Expect manual-intensive processes for these platforms until they mature.

This consideration appears under the broader **Emerging Considerations** note: the listed topics are active areas of evolution, and implementation teams should monitor these and incorporate them into governance processes as practices mature.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When integrating third-party agent platforms with AICT, do not assume they support automated discovery or SDLC-based migration with corporate gate provisions. Plan for manual-intensive processes to bring agents from these platforms under governance until the platforms reach enterprise readiness. Monitor platform maturity over time and fold improved capabilities into governance processes as they become available.
