## What Is This?

This subtopic defines the recommended approach for integrating AICT into an organization's existing governance lifecycle. For organizations that already operate an Architecture Review Board (ARB) and release management processes, AICT should be wired into two key gates so that AI asset details are captured early and validated before go-live. It establishes where governance touchpoints belong relative to deployment.

---

## Two Governance Gates

For organizations with established Architecture Review Board (ARB) and release management processes, AICT should be integrated at two key gates. This approach ensures AI asset details are captured early, before deployment, and validated as complete before go-live.

The two gates are:

1. **Gate 1: Architecture Review Board (Planning Stage)** — where the AI initiative intent is captured early, before deployment.
2. **Gate 2: Release Management (Go-Live)** — where AI asset details are validated as complete before go-live.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers should map AICT touchpoints to their existing ARB and release management processes rather than creating a parallel workflow. Use Gate 1 (ARB / planning stage) to capture AI asset intent and details early, and Gate 2 (release management / go-live) to validate completeness before deployment. This requires that an ARB and a release management process already exist; the two-gate model layers AICT governance onto those established checkpoints.
