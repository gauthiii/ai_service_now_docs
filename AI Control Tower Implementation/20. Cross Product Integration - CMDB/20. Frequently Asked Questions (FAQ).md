## What Is This?

This FAQ explains how the governance components of AI Control Tower connect together — from the regulatory framework at the top down to the individual risk record that gets scored for residual risk at the bottom. It answers how Authority Documents, Citations, Control Objectives, Risk Statements, and Individual Risks relate to one another, helping implementers understand why configuration must happen in a specific order.

## Govern - Risk and Control

### How Authority Documents, Citations, Control Objectives, Risk Statements, and Individual Risks Connect

This explains the full chain - from the regulatory framework sitting at the top, all the way down to the individual risk record that gets scored for residual risk at the bottom.

The chain flows as follows:

- **AUTHORITY DOCUMENT** — (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO Standards)
  - ↓
- **CITATIONS** — (EU AI Act Article 14, NIST AI RMF GOVERN 1.1)
  - ↓
- **CONTROL OBJECTIVES** (What you must DO) ←→ **RISK STATEMENTS** (What could go WRONG)
  - Both mapped to AI System via Impact Assessment
  - ↓
- **INDIVIDUAL RISK RECORDS** — (one per risk statement per AI System)
  - ↓
- **RISK ASSESSMENT** — (inherent × control effectiveness = residual risk per record)

Every governance requirement traces back through the same chain. Understanding this chain helps explain why configuration happens in a specific order and why each component depends on the ones above it.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring AI governance in AI Control Tower, set up components in top-down order: define Authority Documents (regulatory frameworks) first, then their Citations, then the paired Control Objectives and Risk Statements. Map both Control Objectives and Risk Statements to the AI System via an Impact Assessment. This produces Individual Risk Records (one per risk statement per AI System), which are then scored in a Risk Assessment where residual risk = inherent risk × control effectiveness. Because each component depends on those above it, the order of configuration is fixed by this chain.
