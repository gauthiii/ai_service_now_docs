---

## What Is This?

Regulatory Gaps addresses the limitations of using existing regulatory frameworks as the basis for AI governance controls in AICT. It clarifies that current standards such as ISO 42001 (2023) offer governance guidance but predate the current pace of AI development, and therefore cannot be implemented through automation as written. It fits into implementation by setting expectations on how regulatory frameworks should be treated when configuring AI governance.

---

## Regulatory Gaps Guidance

ISO 42001 (2023) provides useful governance guidance but was written before the current pace of AI development and cannot be implemented through automation as written. Supplement OOTB EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF content with organization-specific controls. Treat regulatory frameworks as guidance baselines, not complete implementations.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring AI governance in AICT, do not rely on regulatory frameworks alone as a complete control implementation. Use the out-of-the-box EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF content as a starting point, and supplement it with organization-specific controls to close gaps that the frameworks do not cover. Treat ISO 42001 (2023) and similar standards as guidance baselines rather than automatable, complete implementations.
