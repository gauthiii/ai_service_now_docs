## What Is This?

Gate 1 is the first of two recommended governance gates for integrating AICT into an organization's existing delivery process. It positions the Architecture Review Board (ARB), during the planning stage, as the point where the intent behind an AI initiative is captured. This gate ensures AI asset details are recorded early, before deployment, complementing what automated discovery alone can surface.

---

## Gate 1: Architecture Review Board (Planning Stage)

The Architecture Review Board (ARB) is where the AI initiative intent is captured. Discovery cannot provide intent: it can surface what exists, but cannot answer why an asset was built, what business outcome it serves, or what its risk profile is. The ARB gate closes that gap.

- Identify which business applications have AI in them, or which platforms are acting as AI hosts
- Capture AICT details early: models, prompts, datasets, and integration patterns

### Context: Recommended Process Flow — Two Governance Gates

For organizations with established Architecture Review Board (ARB) and release management processes, AICT should be integrated at two key gates. This approach ensures AI asset details are captured early, before deployment, and validated as complete before go-live. Gate 1 (this subtopic) is the ARB/planning-stage gate; a second gate occurs at release management before go-live.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers should embed AICT capture into the existing ARB review at the planning stage. When an AI initiative comes before the ARB, use the gate to: (1) identify which business applications contain AI or which platforms act as AI hosts, and (2) capture AICT details early — models, prompts, datasets, and integration patterns — so the intent (why the asset was built, the business outcome it serves, and its risk profile) is recorded before deployment, since discovery alone cannot supply this. This sets up the assets to later move from Unmanaged to Managed governance state and prepares them for the second governance gate at go-live.
