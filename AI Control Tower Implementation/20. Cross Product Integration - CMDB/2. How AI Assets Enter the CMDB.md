## What Is This?

This subtopic explains the paths through which AI configuration items (CIs) are created in the ServiceNow CMDB. It identifies the discovery and registration methods used by AI Control Tower (AICT) and notes that the chosen method determines both the timing of CI creation and whether the CI enters as managed or unmanaged. It fits within the broader Cross Product Integration - CMDB section that maps AI assets to CMDB classes.

---

## Paths for AI CI Creation

AI CIs are created through five paths:

- Automated discovery (scheduled or event-driven)
- Connector-based discovery for hyperscaler platforms
- Manual intake via the AICT Workspace
- Programmatic API registration

The discovery method determines when the CI is created and whether it enters as managed or unmanaged.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When integrating AICT with the CMDB, implementers select among the available creation paths based on their environment: rely on automated discovery (scheduled or event-driven) for ongoing detection, connector-based discovery for hyperscaler platforms, manual intake through the AICT Workspace for human-initiated registration, and API registration for programmatic onboarding. The choice of discovery method affects the timing of CI creation and the managed/unmanaged status of the resulting CI, which should be considered when planning governance over these records.
