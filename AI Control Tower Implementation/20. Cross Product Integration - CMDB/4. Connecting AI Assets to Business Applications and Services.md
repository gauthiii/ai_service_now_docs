## What Is This?

This subtopic describes how AI Configuration Items (CIs) in the CMDB are linked to the business applications, services, and owning teams they relate to, using the Common Service Data Model (CSDM). These relationships make the business context of each AI asset visible directly in the AICT Workspace, alongside its governance status, evaluation scores, and security posture.

---

## Linking AI CIs Through CSDM

AI CIs are linked to business applications, services, and owning teams through CSDM. These relationships are visible directly in the AICT Workspace alongside governance status, evaluation scores, and security posture.

Key relationships in the CSDM model:

- AI systems are mapped to the business applications they power
- Models are linked to their parent AI systems
- Datasets, prompts, and knowledge bases are linked to the models that consume them
- All assets are associated with owning teams and business units

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

To apply this in implementation, ensure AI CIs are onboarded into the CMDB and mapped through CSDM so each AI system ties to its business application, models tie to their parent AI systems, and datasets/prompts/knowledge bases tie to the models that consume them. Associate every asset with its owning team and business unit. Once these CSDM relationships exist, implementers and AI Stewards can review business context together with governance status, evaluation scores, and security posture from a single view in the AICT Workspace.
