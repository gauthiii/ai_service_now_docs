# Managed vs. Unmanaged Assets

> ServiceNow AI Control Tower (AICT) — Implementation Guide
> Section: Cross Product Integration - CMDB → Managed vs. Unmanaged Assets
> Source: AI-Control-Tower.pdf, p.238

---

## What Is This?

Within AI Control Tower (AICT), every Configuration Item (CI) exists in one of two governance states: managed or unmanaged. This subtopic explains that the governance state of an asset is what determines which capabilities apply to it. It is part of the Cross Product Integration with CMDB, governing how AI assets are tracked and operated on once they enter the CMDB.

---

## Governance States

Every CI in AICT exists in one of two governance states. The distinction determines which workflows, assessments, and measurement capabilities are active for that asset.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When onboarding AI assets into AICT, implementers should be aware that an asset's governance state (managed vs. unmanaged) controls which workflows, assessments, and measurement capabilities are active for it. Newly discovered assets (for example, those created via Hyperscaler Connectors) appear as unmanaged until governance onboarding is complete, so part of the implementation process is moving assets into the managed state to activate the full set of governance workflows.
