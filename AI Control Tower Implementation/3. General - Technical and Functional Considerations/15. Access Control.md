## What Is This?

Access Control in AI Control Tower defines the out-of-the-box roles that govern who can manage AI governance, risk, case handling, and asset oversight. These roles are provisioned to groups aligned to a given persona, so that the right users gain the permissions and workspace views appropriate to their responsibilities. This is a foundational step in implementation to ensure each persona can act within AICT.

---

## Overview

AI Control Tower ships with a set of out-of-the-box roles designed to support the key personas involved in AI governance, risk management, case handling, and asset oversight. These roles are applied to groups aligned to a given persona — for example, a group containing AI stewards would be provisioned the AI Steward role.

> **Tip:** AI risk and compliance details are accessible under the Risk and Compliance tab in the AICT workspace. Ensure role assignments align with workspace configuration to avoid personas landing in views they cannot act on.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing AICT, identify the personas in your organization (AI stewards, asset owners, risk and compliance administrators, workspace users) and create or align groups for each. Provision the corresponding out-of-the-box role to each group using its scope ID rather than assigning roles to individual users. Confirm that role assignments align with the AICT workspace configuration — especially the Risk and Compliance tab — so personas do not land in views they cannot act on. Note that the AI Asset Owner role carries additional platform roles (Assessment Admin, SAE administrator) and is auto-assigned Deploy phase tasks.
