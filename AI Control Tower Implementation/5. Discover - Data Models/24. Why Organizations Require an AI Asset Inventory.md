---

## Why Organizations Require an AI Asset Inventory

As AI adoption scales, organizations require a centralized mechanism to govern AI usage across business functions.

AI Asset Inventory enables:

- Centralized tracking of AI systems and use cases
- Visibility into models, datasets, and decision logic
- Alignment with governance, risk, and compliance frameworks

It supports:

- AI Product Owners in lifecycle management
- AI Stewards / CoE in governance enforcement
- Risk & Compliance teams in identifying and mitigating risk

**Outcome:** A single source of truth for AI, enabling transparency, accountability, and regulatory readiness.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

Implementers should treat the AI Asset Inventory as the foundational data model that consolidates AI systems, use cases, models, datasets, and decision logic into one governed repository. By centralizing this information, the inventory aligns the AI estate with governance, risk, and compliance frameworks and provides each stakeholder group—AI Product Owners, AI Stewards / CoE, and Risk & Compliance teams—the visibility they need for lifecycle management, governance enforcement, and risk mitigation. The end goal is a single source of truth that supports transparency, accountability, and regulatory readiness.
