## What Is This?

AI Systems is one of the Core AI Asset Types tracked within the AI Control Tower data model. It represents engineered or machine-based solutions that perform tasks such as decision-making, prediction, content generation, or analysis. AI Systems sit at the top of the AI Asset Definitions and can be composed of multiple interconnected subsystems, which AICT tracks to support auditability and risk assessments.

---

## AI Systems Definition

AI systems are engineered or machine-based solutions that perform tasks such as decision-making, prediction, content generation, or analysis.

**Key characteristics:**

- Operate with varying levels of autonomy
- May learn and adapt over time
- Can be composed of multiple interconnected subsystems

**Hierarchical Systems** — AI systems may depend on other systems (sub-systems). Capturing these relationships enables:

- Accountability tracking
- Dependency mapping
- Risk propagation analysis

> **Note**
> AI Control Tower tracks system relationships to support auditability and risk assessments.

## Context: Core AI Asset Types and Supporting Data Structures

AI Systems is the first of the Core AI Asset Types. For context, the full set is:

**Core AI Asset Types:**

- AI Systems
- AI Models
- AI Datasets
- MCP Servers
- AI Prompts

**Supporting Data Structures:**

- Inputs / Outputs – Defines how AI systems interact with data
- AI Tools – Capabilities enabling agentic execution (linked to systems, not standalone assets)
- Model Lineage – Tracks model evolution, training datasets, and evaluation history

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When modeling AI assets in the AI Control Tower, represent each engineered or machine-based solution as an AI System record. Because AI systems can be composed of interconnected subsystems and may depend on other systems, capture these hierarchical relationships so the platform can support accountability tracking, dependency mapping, and risk propagation analysis. Maintaining these system relationships is what enables AICT's auditability and risk assessment capabilities. AI Systems link to other asset types (AI Models, AI Tools, Inputs/Outputs) rather than existing in isolation.
