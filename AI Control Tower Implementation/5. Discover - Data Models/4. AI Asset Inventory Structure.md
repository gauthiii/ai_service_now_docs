## What Is This?

The AI Asset Inventory Structure defines the set of components that the AI Asset Inventory catalogs in order to deliver AI-enabled functionality. It organizes governed assets into core AI asset types plus the supporting data structures that connect them. This structure is foundational to enabling traceability, risk management, and lifecycle governance across AI initiatives within the AI Control Tower.

---

## AI Asset Inventory Structure

The AI Asset Inventory catalogs all components required to deliver AI-enabled functionality. Organizations may govern one or more of the following asset types.

### Core AI Asset Types

- AI Systems
- AI Models
- AI Datasets
- MCP Servers
- AI Prompts

### Supporting Data Structures

- **Inputs / Outputs** – Defines how AI systems interact with data
- **AI Tools** – Capabilities enabling agentic execution (linked to systems, not standalone assets)
- **Model Lineage** – Tracks model evolution, training datasets, and evaluation history

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing the AI Asset Inventory, decide which of the five core asset types (AI Systems, AI Models, AI Datasets, MCP Servers, AI Prompts) your organization needs to govern, since not all need apply. Beyond the core assets, account for the supporting data structures: model Inputs/Outputs that define data interaction, AI Tools that are linked to systems rather than catalogued as standalone assets, and Model Lineage that tracks model evolution and training/evaluation history. Capturing these consistently is what enables the inventory to support traceability, risk management, and lifecycle governance.
