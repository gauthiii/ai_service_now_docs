## What Is This?

AI Asset Definitions describe the core asset types that AI Control Tower discovers, tracks, and governs within the data model. This subtopic defines each AI asset type — starting with AI Systems and AI Models — including their key characteristics and the relationships they participate in. It establishes the vocabulary and structure that underpins auditability and risk assessment across the platform.

---

## AI Systems

AI systems are engineered or machine-based solutions that perform tasks such as decision-making, prediction, content generation, or analysis.

Key characteristics:

- Operate with varying levels of autonomy
- May learn and adapt over time
- Can be composed of multiple interconnected subsystems

Hierarchical Systems - AI systems may depend on other systems (sub-systems). Capturing these relationships enables:

- Accountability tracking
- Dependency mapping
- Risk propagation analysis

> **Note**
> AI Control Tower tracks system relationships to support auditability and risk assessments.

---

## AI Models

AI models are the computational engines powering AI systems. They are trained on datasets to perform specific tasks such as classification, prediction, or generation.

Common training approaches:

- Supervised learning

## How to Use This in Implementation

When modeling AI assets in AI Control Tower, register each AI System as a discrete asset and capture its dependencies on any sub-systems to enable accountability tracking, dependency mapping, and risk propagation analysis. Associate AI Models with the systems they power and record their training approach and datasets, so the platform can maintain auditability and support risk assessments across the asset hierarchy.
