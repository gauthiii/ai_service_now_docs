## What Is This?

AI tools are capabilities that enable AI systems—particularly agentic systems—to perform actions. Within the AICT data model, tools are treated as attributes of AI systems rather than independently governed assets. They are surfaced through integrations and do not carry their own lifecycle governance inside AICT.

---

## AI Tools (Agentic Context)

AI tools are capabilities that enable AI systems—particularly agentic systems—to perform actions.

- Tools are attributes of AI systems, not standalone governed assets
- They are discovered through integrations (e.g., AWS, Azure)
- They do not have independent lifecycle governance within AICT

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When modeling agentic AI systems in AICT, capture tools as part of the parent AI system's attributes rather than registering them as separate governed assets. Rely on integration-based discovery (such as AWS or Azure connectors) to surface available tools. Because tools have no independent lifecycle governance in AICT, governance, risk, and compliance oversight should be applied at the AI system level that owns those tools.
