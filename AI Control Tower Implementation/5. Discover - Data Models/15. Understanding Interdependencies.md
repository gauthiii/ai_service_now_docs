## What Is This?

Understanding Interdependencies describes how AI governance requires the explicit mapping of relationships between the core AI data model entities — AI Systems, AI Models, and Datasets. Within the AICT data model, capturing these connections ensures end-to-end traceability and complete governance coverage. It fits into the Discover phase, where each AI capability is inventoried and linked to its supporting components.

---

## Mapping Relationships Between AI Data Model Entities

AI governance requires explicit mapping of relationships between:

- AI System (business function)
- AI Model (decision logic)
- Dataset (training and evaluation source)

### Consequences of Not Capturing Relationships

Failure to capture these relationships can lead to:

- Incomplete risk assessments
- Gaps in audit readiness
- Weak compliance alignment

---

## Best Practice

For each AI capability:

1. Inventory the AI system
2. Identify associated models
3. Document datasets used for training and evaluation

This structured mapping ensures end-to-end traceability and governance coverage.

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When discovering and modeling AI capabilities in AICT, implementers should not treat AI Systems, AI Models, and Datasets as isolated records. For each AI capability, inventory the AI system (the business function), identify the associated AI models (the decision logic), and document the datasets used for training and evaluation. Establishing these links is a prerequisite for complete risk assessments, audit readiness, and compliance alignment — omitting them leaves governance coverage incomplete.
