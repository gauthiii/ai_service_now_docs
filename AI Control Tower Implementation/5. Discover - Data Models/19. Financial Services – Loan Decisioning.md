## What Is This?

This subtopic is the first of several industry examples that illustrate how AI assets manifest across industries to help properly configure the AI Asset Inventory. It presents a Financial Services scenario in which AI is used for loan decisioning, showing how AI Systems, Models, Datasets, MCP Servers, and related assets are represented in the inventory. It fits within the Discover phase, where AI assets are catalogued and modeled.

---

## Scenario

**Scenario:** A retail bank uses AI to automate home loan approvals.

This example introduces the broader set of "Examples of AI Asset Inventory by Industry, Use Case, and AI Type." Understanding how AI assets manifest across industries is critical to properly configuring the AI Asset Inventory. The examples illustrate how AI Systems, Models, Datasets, Prompts, Inputs, and Outputs are represented across different domains and AI types.

### Diagram/Screenshot: Loan Application and Approvals AI Asset Hierarchy

The diagram on this page shows a hierarchical breakdown of a loan-decisioning AI System into its constituent assets:

- **Top level — AI System:** "Loan application and approvals agent." Description: "Consists of models, interfaces, monitoring tools, decision rules, APIs, and more."
- **Middle level — three branches under the AI System:**
  - **AI Model: ServiceNow** — Now LLM
  - **AI Model: Open AI** — GPT 5.0
  - **MCP Servers: Credit Bureau** — Fico scores
- **Bottom level — a Dataset under each middle node:**
  - Under ServiceNow → **Dataset: Financial Info.** — Income levels, Payment history, etc.
  - Under Open AI → **Dataset: Public Records** — Bankruptcy filings, Court judgments, etc.
  - Under Credit Bureau → **Dataset: Financial Behavior.** — Spending pattern, Savings habits, etc.

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring the AI Asset Inventory for a loan-decisioning use case, model the overall solution as a single AI System ("Loan application and approvals agent") and link its component assets beneath it: the AI Models (e.g., ServiceNow Now LLM, OpenAI GPT 5.0), any MCP Servers (e.g., a Credit Bureau providing FICO scores), and the Datasets each consumes (Financial Info, Public Records, Financial Behavior). Use this structure to ensure models, interfaces, decision rules, APIs, and their underlying datasets are all represented and traceable. This example pattern can be adapted to other industries and AI types when populating the inventory.
