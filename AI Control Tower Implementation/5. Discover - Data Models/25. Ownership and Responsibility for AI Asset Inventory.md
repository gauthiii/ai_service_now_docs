## What Is This?

This subtopic defines who is accountable for maintaining the AI Asset Inventory within an organization. It identifies the roles responsible for governance oversight and data accuracy, and describes the operational reality of how AI asset data is typically managed before centralization. It fits into the Discover phase, where AI assets are catalogued and ownership is assigned.

---

## Ownership and Responsibility

Responsibility for maintaining the AI Asset Inventory typically resides with:

- AI Stewards / AI CoE (governance oversight)
- AI Asset Owners (data accuracy and lifecycle updates)

---

## Operational Reality

AI asset data is often:

- Distributed across multiple tools and platforms
- Siloed across teams (IT, Data Science, Risk, Legal)
- Stored in informal repositories (spreadsheets, email, shared drives)

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When standing up the AI Asset Inventory, assign clear ownership: designate AI Stewards or the AI Center of Excellence (CoE) for governance oversight, and AI Asset Owners for keeping data accurate and current through lifecycle updates. Implementers should account for the operational reality that AI asset data is initially distributed, siloed across IT, Data Science, Risk, and Legal teams, and held in informal repositories such as spreadsheets, email, and shared drives — consolidating these sources into a single inventory is a key migration challenge.
