## What Is This?

This subtopic summarizes the key implementation considerations for establishing and maintaining the AI Asset Inventory data model in AI Control Tower. It outlines the foundational practices an implementer should adopt to ensure AI assets are consistently captured, owned, populated, traced, and governed. It sits at the close of the Discover / Data Models section before moving into industry-specific inventory examples.

---

## Implementation Considerations

- Establish standardized intake processes for all AI assets
- Enforce role-based ownership across lifecycle stages
- Integrate discovery sources (e.g., AWS, Azure) for automated population
- Maintain lineage tracking for models and datasets
- Align inventory data with AI Risk & Compliance frameworks (e.g., EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF)

## How to Use This in Implementation

When configuring the AI Asset Inventory data model, an implementer should: define standardized intake processes so every AI asset (systems, models, datasets, MCP servers) is captured consistently; assign role-based ownership tied to each lifecycle stage; connect external discovery sources such as AWS and Azure to auto-populate the inventory; preserve lineage between models and their datasets (as shown in the AI System hierarchy); and ensure the captured inventory data maps to AI Risk & Compliance frameworks like the EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF. The hierarchy diagram serves as a reference template for decomposing a real AI System into its constituent models, MCP servers, and datasets during inventory configuration.
