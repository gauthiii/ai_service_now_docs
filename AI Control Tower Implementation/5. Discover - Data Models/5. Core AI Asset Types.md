---

## What Is This?

Core AI Asset Types define the primary categories of AI assets that AI Control Tower discovers and manages within its data model. Alongside these core asset types, supporting data structures describe how AI systems interact with data and how their relationships and history are tracked. This forms the foundational inventory layer for governance, auditability, and risk assessment.

---

## Core AI Asset Types

The core AI asset types are:

- AI Systems
- AI Models
- AI Datasets
- MCP Servers
- AI Prompts

---

## Supporting Data Structures

Supporting data structures complement the core asset types:

- **Inputs / Outputs** – Defines how AI systems interact with data
- **AI Tools** – Capabilities enabling agentic execution (linked to systems, not standalone assets)
- **Model Lineage** – Tracks model evolution, training datasets, and evaluation history

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When implementing AI Control Tower discovery, treat AI Systems, AI Models, AI Datasets, MCP Servers, and AI Prompts as the core inventoried asset types. Model the supporting data structures (Inputs/Outputs, AI Tools, and Model Lineage) as relationships and tracking constructs rather than standalone assets — note that AI Tools are linked to systems, not registered independently. Capturing these asset types and their relationships establishes the inventory needed for accountability, dependency mapping, and risk assessment.
