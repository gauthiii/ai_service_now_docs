## What Is This?

Supporting Data Structures are the auxiliary data constructs in the AI Control Tower data model that complement the Core AI Asset Types (AI Systems, AI Models, AI Datasets, MCP Servers, AI Prompts). They capture how AI systems interact with data, the capabilities that enable agentic execution, and the evolution of models over time. These structures provide the relational and historical context needed for auditability, dependency mapping, and governance.

---

## Supporting Data Structures

The AI Control Tower data model defines the following supporting data structures:

- **Inputs / Outputs** – Defines how AI systems interact with data
- **AI Tools** – Capabilities enabling agentic execution (linked to systems, not standalone assets)
- **Model Lineage** – Tracks model evolution, training datasets, and evaluation history

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

When modeling AI assets in AI Control Tower, use these supporting structures alongside the core asset types. Capture Inputs/Outputs to document data interactions for each AI system, link AI Tools to their parent systems rather than treating them as standalone assets, and maintain Model Lineage to preserve the history of model evolution, training datasets, and evaluations. These structures support traceability and governance across the AI estate.
