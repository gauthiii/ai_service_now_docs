## What Is This?

This subtopic defines how to scope an AI asset inventory using a structured, governance-driven approach. It establishes the criteria for what counts as an AI asset, how use cases map to AI Systems, the boundaries of inventory coverage, and how the inventory integrates with broader enterprise systems. It is a foundational discovery step that prevents fragmentation and supports complete, audit-ready asset records.

---

## Determining the Scope of AI Asset Inventory

A structured, governance-driven approach is required to define scope.

### 1. Define AI Assets

Establish clear criteria based on:

- AI technology type (ML, NLP, GenAI, Agentic AI)
- Organizational standards
- Regulatory requirements

### 2. Classify AI Use Cases as AI Systems

Each use case should be mapped to an AI System based on:

- Business purpose
- Criticality and impact
- Regulatory exposure

### 3. Establish Inventory Scope

Define:

- Asset types to be tracked
- Source systems and platforms
- Lifecycle coverage (intake → build → deploy → retire)

### 4. Integrate with Enterprise Systems

Ensure alignment with:

- CRM, ITSM, HR systems
- Vendor and procurement systems
- Software asset management tools

---

## How to Use This in Implementation

An implementer follows the four-step sequence to bound the AI asset inventory before discovery begins. First, set criteria to define what qualifies as an AI asset (by technology type, organizational standards, and regulatory requirements). Next, map each AI use case to an AI System using business purpose, criticality/impact, and regulatory exposure. Then fix the inventory scope by deciding the asset types, source systems/platforms, and lifecycle coverage from intake through retirement. Finally, align the inventory with enterprise systems (CRM, ITSM, HR, vendor/procurement, and software asset management tools) so records stay complete and consistent rather than fragmented.
